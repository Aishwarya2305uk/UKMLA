How long does GMC registration take? For most doctors — whether a UK medical graduate moving from the Foundation Programme onto the register or an international medical graduate (IMG) coming through the UKMLA route — the honest answer is that GMC registration itself is usually the fastest single stage of a much longer journey. The registration application, once every piece of supporting evidence is in place, is typically decided within a matter of weeks. The real variable that determines whether someone reaches the register in six months or in two years is everything that happens before that application is submitted: passing the UKMLA or PLAB, having a primary medical qualification verified, meeting the English language requirement, and assembling a complete, query-free evidence file.

This guide is a realistic, sequential timeline for both audiences. It does not promise a single fixed number of weeks, because the GMC's own processing benchmarks are reviewed periodically and vary with application volume — instead, it lays out each stage in order, gives indicative timescales drawn from the GMC's own published guidance and verification-partner data, and flags the two most common, entirely avoidable causes of delay. This article sits above both, focused purely on the timeline question: how long does the whole journey actually take, stage by stage, and where does the time really go?

Quick Answer: How Long Does GMC Registration Take?

GMC registration take roughly speaking breaks into two very different kinds of time: the GMC's own internal processing of a complete application, and everything that has to happen before that application can be submitted. Once a complete, query-free registration application reaches the GMC, published guidance and third-party tracking of real applicants suggest a typical processing window of around two to six weeks for straightforward cases, though the GMC itself states it aims to close most applications within roughly three months and some portfolio-pathway routes can run three to six months. That figure, however, is only one link in a much longer chain.

For a UK medical graduate, the pre-registration steps are relatively short, since primary qualification verification is largely automatic and English language evidence is not usually required. For an IMG, the full journey — from starting UKMLA preparation through to holding full registration with a licence to practise — typically spans somewhere between twelve months and two-and-a-half years, depending heavily on how quickly primary source verification (EPIC) completes and how efficiently the candidate sequences the English language test, exam bookings, and the registration application itself. The single most important thing to understand about how long GMC registration takes is that the GMC's own decision time is usually the shortest part of the journey — the bottleneck is almost always upstream of it.

Because the GMC updates its own indicative processing times periodically, and because volume fluctuates throughout the year, this guide deliberately avoids presenting a single frozen number as permanent fact. Readers should always check the GMC's own live processing-time information on the GMC's registration application page before making firm plans around a specific date, such as a job start date or a travel booking.

Why "How Long Does GMC Registration Take" Doesn't Have One Universal Answer

Search for how long GMC registration takes and it is tempting to want a single number — "eight weeks," "a year," "eighteen months" — that can be dropped straight into a spreadsheet or a family conversation. The honest reality is that no single number can responsibly apply to every reader, because the phrase itself bundles together at least two very different questions: how long does the GMC itself take to process a submitted application, and how long does the entire journey take from a standing start. This guide answers both, deliberately kept separate, because conflating them is one of the most common sources of frustration for candidates partway through the pathway.

It also matters enormously which audience is asking. A newly graduating UK medical student moving into provisional registration ahead of the Foundation Programme faces a fundamentally shorter and more predictable timeline than an IMG starting from scratch overseas, simply because so much of the IMG timeline is consumed by steps — primary source verification, English language testing, exam preparation from a standing start — that a UK graduate either does not face at all or faces in a much lighter form. Finally, it is worth being honest about why this guide presents ranges rather than fixed weekly figures throughout. The GMC's own indicative processing benchmarks are reviewed periodically, application volumes fluctuate across the year, and an individual candidate's circumstances — country of qualification, how organised their evidence is, whether they need a resit — can move their personal timeline substantially in either direction from any average. Stage One: Confirming Eligibility and Qualification Recognition

Eligibility confirmation is the first practical step in the pathway, and it typically takes anywhere from a few days to a few weeks depending on how straightforward the underlying qualification is to check. For UK medical graduates, this stage is close to instantaneous in practice: a UK primary medical qualification from a GMC-recognised medical school is accepted without the extended verification process required of most overseas qualifications. For IMGs, eligibility confirmation means checking that the primary medical qualification (PMQ) is recognised by the GMC as sufficient to enter the UKMLA pathway at all, before any time is invested in exam preparation.

This stage matters because it is the cheapest possible point at which to catch a problem. A candidate who confirms eligibility properly before beginning exam preparation avoids the much costlier scenario of discovering, after months of study, that a qualification issue needs resolving first. The site's dedicated eligibility guide covers this in detail; before beginning any exam preparation, candidates should also check the general registration requirements that apply to international doctors, since these set out the qualification, internship, and character/health requirements common to (almost) every applicant regardless of country of training.

UK Graduates vs. IMGs at This Stage

UK medical graduates typically move from their final year of medical school directly into provisional registration ahead of the Foundation Programme, and this administrative step is comparatively fast because the GMC already recognises UK medical schools' qualifications without needing a separate primary source verification process. IMGs, by contrast, need their qualification, and in most cases their internship record, verified independently — this is the step that later becomes EPIC, covered in detail below. Consequently, while a UK graduate's overall pre-registration timeline is measured in weeks, an IMG's pre-registration timeline is realistically measured in months, and this difference should be built into planning from day one rather than discovered partway through.

Stage Two: Passing the UKMLA (or PLAB)

Passing the UKMLA — the Applied Knowledge Test (AKT) and the Clinical and Professional Skills Assessment (CPSA) — is, for most candidates, the single longest stage of the entire journey, though it runs largely in parallel with the administrative stages rather than strictly before them. Preparation time varies enormously by individual circumstance, prior clinical experience, and study intensity, but a realistic planning range for most candidates preparing from scratch is somewhere between six and eighteen months of study before attempting the AKT, with the CPSA typically following once the AKT has been passed.

It is worth being explicit that this stage is not simply "time spent studying" — it also includes exam booking lead times, which vary by sitting window and test-centre location, and the possibility of a resit if either component is not passed on the first attempt. Candidates who fail a component need to factor in the wait until the next available sitting, plus any mandatory gap the GMC's exam regulations impose between attempts, which can meaningfully extend the overall timeline. Because of this, treating "pass both components" as a single fixed-duration block understates the real variability involved; a realistic timeline builds in some contingency for at least one resit, even for well-prepared candidates.

Why This Stage Should Run in Parallel, Not in Sequence

A recurring mistake among IMGs specifically is treating UKMLA preparation and the administrative track (English testing, primary source verification) as sequential — study first, then start the paperwork once exams are passed. This is almost always the slower approach. Because EPIC verification and English language testing depend on external response times rather than personal study effort, there is no efficiency gained by delaying them until exams are finished; every week spent waiting to start the paperwork is a week added to the overall timeline that could otherwise have run invisibly in the background while studying continued. The most time-efficient candidates begin the administrative track during, not after, their exam preparation period.

Stage Three: Meeting the English Language Requirement

The English language requirement applies to the great majority of IMGs (UK graduates are generally exempt, having studied their primary qualification in English in the UK) and is satisfied most commonly through an approved IELTS or OET certificate meeting the GMC's minimum score bands. Booking and sitting the test itself typically takes a matter of weeks from decision to certificate in hand, assuming a test centre with reasonably near-term availability, but the detail that catches candidates out is not the test itself — it is the certificate's validity window.

GMC-approved English language test certificates are generally treated as valid for two years from the test date. If a candidate sits their English test early in their pathway and then experiences a long delay elsewhere — most often in primary source verification — there is a genuine risk that the certificate expires before the rest of the application is ready to submit, forcing a costly and entirely avoidable resit. This single issue is one of the two delay risks flagged prominently later in this guide, precisely because it is so common and so preventable with basic sequencing awareness.

What Happens If a Certificate Expires Before Registration

If an English language certificate lapses before the rest of the evidence is ready, the GMC may, in some circumstances, accept alternative evidence of maintained English proficiency — such as a reference confirming recent postgraduate study or clinical employment conducted in English — or may in other cases require the test to be resat entirely. Because the exact criteria for an extension or alternative evidence can change and depend on individual circumstances, candidates approaching the two-year mark without a completed application should check current GMC guidance directly rather than assume an exception will automatically apply to their situation.

Stage Four: Primary Source Verification (EPIC) for Applicable Countries

Primary source verification, run through EPIC (the Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials) for most overseas-qualified doctors, is the step most likely to become the true bottleneck of the entire pathway — and it is also the stage over which a candidate personally has the least direct control, because the verification request is answered by the medical school, not by the candidate. Once an EPIC application is submitted correctly, the initial account and identification-form setup is usually resolved within a couple of days, but the substantive verification — the medical school confirming degree, transcript, and often internship details directly with EPIC's verification team — is where the real variability sits.

Indicative timescales published by verification services and reported by past applicants suggest a typical range of around four to twelve weeks for the full EPIC process once submitted, though some well-organised institutions respond faster and others, particularly those without a dedicated international-verification office, can take three months or considerably longer. Because this range is so wide, and because it depends entirely on institutional administrative speed rather than the candidate's own effort, EPIC verification should be started as early as possible in the overall pathway — ideally alongside the very start of exam preparation, not after.

Why EPIC Cannot Be Rushed or Substituted

A certified copy of a degree certificate only confirms that a photocopy matches an original a candidate holds; it says nothing about whether the issuing institution itself confirms the degree was genuinely awarded. EPIC verification requires the institution to confirm details independently from its own records, communicated directly to the verification platform rather than relayed through the candidate. This is precisely why the process cannot be shortcut by paying an expedited fee or presenting a more elaborately notarised document — the bottleneck is institutional response time, not paperwork presentation, and no amount of personal urgency changes how quickly an overseas academic office processes its queue.

The same underlying EPIC mechanism applies across source countries, even though national regulator specifics and typical response patterns differ.

Stage Five: Internship and PMQ Documentation

Alongside EPIC's verification of the degree itself, most IMGs must also provide evidence of a completed internship or equivalent supervised clinical practice period following their primary medical qualification. This is where the internship requirement for GMC registration becomes relevant, and it is worth understanding on its own terms rather than assuming a degree certificate alone is sufficient evidence of qualification — the GMC treats completed internship documentation as a distinct and necessary piece of evidence, separate from academic transcripts.

This stage runs in parallel with EPIC rather than after it, and the practical advice here is straightforward: gather internship completion certificates, rotation logs, or supervisor confirmations as early as possible, ideally into a single well-organised digital folder alongside the rest of the evidence bundle, rather than waiting for a platform to request each document individually. Doctors who trained through a locally employed or trust-grade route before pursuing full registration should also be aware of how that experience is treated; the site's guide to the locally employed doctors' route to GMC registration explains how clinical service in a non-training post can factor into the overall evidence picture.

Common Documentation Gaps

Two documentation gaps recur across nationalities. First, some medical schools issue transcripts that summarise overall results rather than providing the detailed, year-by-year breakdown the GMC's verification process expects — requesting the most detailed transcript format available, rather than the standard summary given to graduates, avoids a follow-up request later. Second, internship documentation is sometimes incomplete where a rotation was split across multiple hospitals or supervisors, requiring a candidate to track down confirmation from more than one source — starting this process early gives enough runway to chase down a slow-to-respond former supervisor without it becoming a critical-path delay.

Stage Six: Submitting the Registration Application

Once eligibility is confirmed, both UKMLA components are passed, English language evidence is valid and uploaded, and primary source verification plus internship documentation are complete, the registration application itself is submitted through GMC Registration Online. This stage is, in relative terms, the fast part: assuming every piece of supporting evidence is already gathered and valid, the application itself can be completed in a single sitting, covering personal details, health and character declarations, identity verification arrangements, and payment of the registration fee.

The critical planning point here is sequencing, not effort. A candidate who has done the work of keeping every certificate current and every document complete arrives at this stage able to submit immediately; a candidate who has let an English certificate lapse, or who is still waiting on an outstanding EPIC confirmation, effectively pauses the entire pathway at the final hurdle. This is why the stages above are worth treating as a checklist to complete in parallel well in advance, rather than a sequence to work through one at a time as each becomes urgent.

Identity Verification

Identity verification is a standard part of the registration application and, for candidates still based overseas, is often most efficiently combined with a UK trip already planned for the CPSA rather than treated as a separate journey. Confirming exactly which identity verification route applies — some circumstances allow an approved remote option, though combining it with an existing UK visit is usually still the more efficient choice — well before finalising travel plans avoids the need for a second, dedicated trip purely for this purpose.

Stage Seven: The GMC's Own Processing Time

This is the one stage of the entire pathway that the GMC directly controls, and it is deliberately treated separately here because it is the part of the timeline this guide will not attempt to pin to a single permanent number. The GMC publishes its own current indicative processing benchmarks, and these are reviewed and adjusted periodically based on application volume, staffing, and the complexity mix of applications received at any given time. Always check the GMC's live processing-time information directly on the GMC's own registration application page before relying on any fixed figure, including the indicative ranges given elsewhere in this guide.

That said, it is useful to know the shape of what past guidance and applicant experience suggest. For a complete, query-free application, decisions have historically often come back within a small number of weeks; the GMC's own published aim across registration types is generally to close the large majority of applications within around three months of submission, and some routes — including certain specialist and portfolio pathway applications — can run three to six months where additional assessment is involved. Applications that generate a query from the GMC, by contrast, can see their processing time extend meaningfully while the query is resolved, which is precisely why the "how to avoid delays" section below treats a clean, complete first submission as the single highest-leverage action available to a candidate at this stage.

What a GMC Query Means and How It Affects Timing

A query from the GMC during processing is not automatically a sign that something has gone seriously wrong — it is a routine occurrence for a meaningful proportion of applicants, and it usually means one specific piece of evidence needs clarifying rather than the whole application being in doubt. Common triggers include an ambiguous or incomplete transcript, a name discrepancy across documents that has not been formally explained, or a translation that does not meet the required certification standard. The most effective response is prompt, complete, and precisely targeted — providing exactly what has been asked for, in the format requested, since an incomplete response risks triggering a second query and a further, avoidable delay.

How Long Does GMC Registration Take Overall? Putting the Stages Together

Bringing every stage together, the honest answer to how long GMC registration takes depends far more on which audience is asking than on any single universal number. For a UK medical graduate moving from final-year exams into provisional registration ahead of the Foundation Programme, the process is comparatively fast — administrative confirmation typically resolves in weeks rather than months, since primary qualification recognition does not require the extended EPIC verification process, and the English language requirement generally does not apply.

For an IMG entering through the UKMLA pathway, a realistic overall range — from beginning exam preparation and starting the administrative track in parallel, through to holding full registration with a licence to practise — is more typically somewhere in the region of twelve months to two-and-a-half years. Candidates whose medical school responds quickly to EPIC, who pass both UKMLA components on the first attempt, and who sequence their English test carefully against the rest of the application can land toward the shorter end of that range; candidates facing a slow-responding academic office, a resit, or a lapsed English certificate can land considerably further out. This is deliberately presented as a wide range rather than a precise prediction, because no single guide can account for the administrative speed of any individual candidate's specific medical school or the GMC's processing capacity at the exact time they apply.

What a realistic range does allow for is better decision-making — around finances, family planning, employment timing, and travel — than an artificially optimistic single figure would. Candidates who plan around a multi-stage, multi-month-to-multi-year horizon experience considerably less financial and psychological strain than those who assume registration will complete in a handful of months and then find themselves recalculating everything when EPIC or an English certificate becomes the unexpected bottleneck.

How to Avoid the Most Common, Self-Inflicted Delays

Most of the delay candidates experience in reaching GMC registration is not caused by the GMC itself, and it is not caused by bad luck — it is caused by two specific, controllable planning mistakes that recur across nationalities and backgrounds. Addressing both proactively is the single most effective way to keep the overall timeline as close to its shorter end as realistically possible.

  • Incomplete or poorly organised PMQ and internship evidence. Transcripts that summarise rather than itemise results, internship documentation split across multiple supervisors or hospitals, and name discrepancies across degree, passport, and national registration records are the leading causes of a GMC query, and queries add weeks to processing. Assembling a complete, internally consistent evidence bundle before submitting anything — not after a platform flags something missing — is the highest-leverage single action available.
  • English language test validity expiring before the rest of the application is ready. Because GMC-recognised English certificates are generally valid for two years, sitting the test too early relative to a slow-moving EPIC verification is a genuinely common and entirely avoidable trap. The fix is straightforward: map out realistic timelines for EPIC and exam completion before booking the English test, so the certificate's two-year window comfortably covers the point at which the full application will actually be submitted.
  • Starting the administrative track only after passing the exams. As covered above, EPIC verification and English testing run on institutional and test-centre timelines independent of exam preparation. Waiting until after both UKMLA components are passed to begin the paperwork adds the full EPIC/English timeline onto the end of the exam timeline instead of running it alongside — often the single biggest avoidable extension of the overall journey.
  • Requesting the wrong certificate from the wrong national regulatory body. Where a source country's medical regulator has changed structure or name (a well-documented example being India's 2020/2021 transition from the Medical Council of India to the National Medical Commission), candidates sometimes request a Certificate of Good Standing from the wrong body entirely, only discovering the mismatch once the GMC queries it. Confirming the exact required document and issuing authority before requesting anything avoids this specific, very preventable delay.
  • Informal or non-conforming document translations. A translation completed by a bilingual friend or relative, however accurate, typically does not meet the GMC's certification standard. Using a recognised professional translation service and confirming its certification format is accepted in advance prevents a late-stage rejection.
  • Leaving identity verification and UK travel planning until the last minute. Since identity verification for overseas candidates is often most efficiently combined with a UK trip already planned for the CPSA, failing to plan this in advance can force an unnecessary second journey purely for identity purposes, adding both time and cost.

Taken together, these six points share a common thread: nearly every avoidable delay in the pathway comes from treating a parallel-track item as a sequential afterthought, or from submitting evidence that is technically present but not quite complete enough to avoid a query. Neither failure mode requires extra money or extraordinary effort to prevent — both require early planning and a genuinely complete evidence bundle at the point of first submission.

How Long Does GMC Registration Take Compared With Other Countries' Pathways?

Context helps candidates judge whether the UK pathway is unusually slow, unusually fast, or broadly comparable to other destinations they might be weighing up. In practice, the underlying shape of the UK route — a licensing exam, primary source verification of the medical qualification, an English language requirement, and a final registration decision — mirrors the structure used by comparable regulators in the United States (via the ECFMG and USMLE), Australia, and Canada, even though the specific timelines, fees, and exam formats differ in detail. None of these systems offer a meaningfully faster route to full licensure for an IMG starting from scratch; primary source verification of an overseas qualification is a near-universal requirement across major English-speaking healthcare systems, not a UK-specific hurdle.

Where the UK route does differ meaningfully from some alternatives is in how early a candidate can start the parallel administrative track relative to finishing exams. Because EPIC verification, English testing, and UKMLA preparation can all run simultaneously rather than strictly in sequence, candidates who understand this from the outset can compress their effective timeline considerably compared with treating each stage as a separate, sequential block — a lever that is not always as clearly available in every comparable overseas system. This is one of the more practically useful pieces of realistic timeline planning any candidate can act on immediately, regardless of where else they might be considering applying.

Budgeting Time Alongside Cost: Why the Two Are Linked

Although this guide is primarily about timing rather than cost, the two are closely connected in practice, because delays are rarely free. An EPIC verification that runs long, an English test that has to be resat because its two-year validity window lapsed, or a UKMLA resit all carry direct financial cost on top of the emotional cost of a longer wait. Building a realistic timeline is therefore not just about knowing when to expect registration — it is also part of building a realistic budget, since every additional month of delay commonly brings additional living costs, potential lost income, and in some cases additional fees.

This is one of the strongest practical arguments for treating the parallel-track approach described throughout this guide as a genuine priority rather than a nice-to-have. A candidate who spends a modest amount of early effort organising documents, checking eligibility, and starting EPIC promptly is very often trading a small amount of upfront administrative effort for a materially shorter, and therefore materially cheaper, overall pathway to GMC registration.

Setting Realistic Expectations With Family, Employers, and Yourself

Because the full IMG pathway to GMC registration can plausibly run past a year even when nothing goes wrong, one of the most practically valuable things a candidate can do early on is set honest expectations with the people around them — family members who may be supporting the process financially or emotionally, or a current employer who may need to plan around an eventual departure. A candidate who communicates a realistic range from the outset, rather than an optimistic single figure, tends to experience considerably less strain when a particular stage runs longer than hoped, since the possibility was already built into everyone's expectations rather than arriving as a surprise.

The same principle applies to self-management throughout the process. Long administrative pathways with externally controlled timescales — waiting on a medical school's EPIC response, waiting on a GMC processing decision — can be genuinely stressful precisely because so much of the waiting is outside the candidate's control. Recognising which parts of the timeline actually respond to personal effort (organising documents, starting stages early, responding to queries promptly) versus which parts do not (institutional response speed, GMC processing capacity) helps direct energy toward the actions that actually shorten the wait, rather than toward unproductive anxiety about the parts that do not.

A Note on Refunds and What Happens If Plans Change

Given how long the overall pathway can run, it is worth candidates knowing in advance what happens if circumstances change partway through — for example, if an application needs to be withdrawn, or if a fee was paid in error. The GMC does operate defined refund arrangements for certain fees and scenarios, though not universally across every stage of the process; candidates who need to understand exactly which fees are refundable, under what circumstances, and how long a refund typically takes to process should see the site's dedicated explainer on the GMC registration refund policy rather than assume a blanket right to a refund applies in every situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does GMC registration take from start to finish?

For UK medical graduates, moving from qualification to provisional registration typically takes weeks, since primary qualification verification is largely automatic. For IMGs coming through the UKMLA pathway, a realistic full journey — from beginning exam preparation and the administrative track through to holding full registration — commonly spans twelve months to two-and-a-half years, with the GMC's own decision on a complete application usually the shortest single stage rather than the longest.

How long does the GMC take to process a registration application once it's submitted?

Indicative figures suggest a complete, query-free application is often decided within a few weeks, though the GMC's own published aim is generally to close most applications within around three months, and some pathways can extend to three to six months. Because these figures are reviewed periodically, always check the GMC's current published processing-time information rather than relying on a fixed number quoted anywhere else, including this guide.

What is the single biggest cause of delay in reaching GMC registration?

Incomplete or poorly organised evidence — particularly summarised rather than itemised academic transcripts, incomplete internship documentation, and name discrepancies across documents — is the most common trigger for a GMC query, and a query meaningfully extends processing time. The second most common cause is an English language test certificate expiring, since these are typically valid for only two years, before the rest of a slower-moving application (most often EPIC verification) is ready.

Can I start my GMC registration application before I've passed both UKMLA components?

No — full registration with a licence to practise can only be granted once both the AKT and CPSA have been passed, alongside completed primary source verification and valid English language evidence where applicable. However, primary source verification (EPIC) and English testing should both begin well before the exams are sat, since they run on independent timelines and are frequently the true bottleneck in the overall pathway rather than the exams themselves.

Does EPIC verification always take the same amount of time?

No — EPIC verification timescales vary considerably depending on how quickly an individual medical school responds to the verification request, since the request is answered by the institution, not the candidate. Indicative ranges commonly cited are around four to twelve weeks for a full verification, though slower-responding institutions can take considerably longer, which is why starting EPIC as early as possible in the overall pathway is consistently the most effective way to prevent it from becoming the critical-path bottleneck.

Is GMC registration faster for UK medical students than for IMGs?

Yes, generally. UK medical graduates typically move into provisional registration in a matter of weeks because their primary qualification is already recognised without needing the extended primary source verification process most overseas qualifications require, and the English language requirement generally does not apply to them. IMGs face a longer overall administrative track because of EPIC verification, English language evidence, and more extensive documentation requirements, even though the GMC's own final decision time is often broadly similar once a complete file is submitted.

What should I do if my application has been with the GMC longer than the published processing time suggests?

First, check GMC Online directly for any outstanding query or request for further information, since most extended waits are caused by an unanswered query rather than a stalled file. If there is no visible query and the wait has meaningfully exceeded the GMC's current published processing benchmark, contacting the GMC directly for a status update through GMC Online is the appropriate next step, rather than assuming nothing can be done or resubmitting documents speculatively.

Conclusion

How long GMC registration takes is best understood as the sum of several sequential stages rather than a single number: eligibility confirmation, passing the UKMLA or PLAB, meeting the English language requirement, completing primary source verification where applicable, assembling complete internship and qualification evidence, submitting the application itself, and finally the GMC's own processing decision. Of all these stages, the GMC's internal processing time is usually the shortest and the most predictable — the real determinants of an overall fast or slow journey are upstream, in how early the administrative track starts and how complete the evidence bundle is on first submission. Candidates who run the exam-preparation and administrative tracks in parallel, keep English certificates valid against a realistic EPIC timeline, and submit a genuinely complete file give themselves the best realistic chance of reaching the shorter end of the range.
