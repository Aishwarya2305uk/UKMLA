Knowing the UKMLA key dates is not optional — it is the backbone of effective planning for both UK medical students and international medical graduates who want to pass the exam on their first attempt and secure a clinical post in the UK without delays. Miss a registration deadline by even a few hours and you could be pushed back by three to six months. This guide lays out every important date, window, and deadline in detail, including the 2025/2026 sitting schedule, how dates differ for UK students versus IMGs, and how to plan backwards from your target start date.

The UKMLA is administered differently for its two main candidate groups. UK medical students have their AKT and CPSA dates set by their medical school as part of the programme, while IMGs apply directly to the GMC and must book their own exam sittings. Understanding which set of dates applies to you is the first step in building your plan.

UKMLA Key Dates for UK Medical Students

For students at UK medical schools, the AKT and CPSA are embedded within the undergraduate curriculum. Your medical school coordinates examination entries and communicates sitting dates internally. While the GMC sets the overall examination windows, your school determines exactly when within that window you sit.

Typically, the AKT is offered in Year 4 or Year 5 of a five-year programme (or Year 3 or Year 4 of a four-year graduate-entry programme). The CPSA is usually sat in the final year, often in the spring term to allow results to feed into graduation and Foundation Programme application processes. However, the exact timing varies by institution, so you must always confirm dates with your own medical school.

The relationship between UKMLA results and FPAS (Foundation Programme Application System) deadlines is critical. The FPAS ranking process takes into account your Educational Performance Measure (EPM), and a strong AKT score can enhance your application. FPAS applications typically open in the autumn of your final year, with allocation outcomes in early spring. If your AKT is scheduled for autumn of your final year and you perform poorly, there may be limited time to resit before FPAS applications close. Check with your school whether an early AKT sitting (in the penultimate year) is available as a buffer.

Typical UK Medical Student Timeline

  • Year 4 / Penultimate year: First AKT sitting offered by most medical schools
  • Year 5 / Final year, autumn: Second AKT sitting (resit if needed, or first for some programmes)
  • Year 5 / Final year, spring: CPSA sitting
  • FPAS application: Typically opens October/November of final year
  • FPAS ranking: Released February/March
  • Foundation post start: August (F1 year begins)

UKMLA Key Dates for IMGs: The PLAB and AKT Route

For IMGs, the most important UKMLA key dates are the PLAB 1 (now transitioning to AKT) sitting dates published by the GMC. As of 2025-2026, the GMC runs AKT/PLAB 1 examinations four times per year: in February, May, August, and November. These sittings happen both in the UK and at selected international test centres.

The 2026 confirmed sitting dates include February 12 and May 21, with August and November sittings expected to follow the same quarterly pattern. The GMC announces dates for the following year towards the end of the current year, so checking your GMC Online account regularly from September/October onwards is advisable if you are planning to sit in the first half of the following year.

Set calendar reminders for the booking opening date and be at your computer ready to book immediately.

How to Book PLAB 1 Internationally: Step by Step

Booking PLAB 1 (or the UKMLA AKT for IMGs) requires a GMC Online account and a completed eligibility check. Here is the process in sequence:

  1. Confirm your eligibility: Your MyIntealth verification must be complete and results received by the GMC before you can book.
  2. Log into GMC Online: Go to the GMC Online portal and log in with your credentials.
  3. Navigate to the exam booking section: Find the PLAB or UKMLA AKT booking section in your GMC Online dashboard.
  4. Select your preferred sitting: Choose the sitting date and test centre that works for you. If your preferred centre is full, check other centres in your country or neighbouring countries.
  5. Complete the booking and pay the fee: The exam fee must be paid at the time of booking. For 2025/2026, PLAB 1 costs £255. Keep your booking confirmation email as your proof of registration.
  6. Wait for your admission ticket: The GMC sends your formal admission letter (also called the exam admission ticket) closer to the exam date, usually two to four weeks before sitting.
  7. Plan travel and logistics: If you are sitting outside your home country, book travel and accommodation after confirming your exam slot — not before.

PLAB 2 Booking Dates After PLAB 1 Pass

Once you pass PLAB 1, you must sit PLAB 2 within two years. PLAB 2 is held exclusively in Manchester, UK, so all international candidates must travel to the UK for this component. PLAB 2 sittings run throughout the year, and the GMC typically releases blocks of dates several months in advance.

The waiting time for a PLAB 2 slot has historically been significant — often four to six months from the date of booking due to demand. Candidates are therefore advised to book PLAB 2 as soon as possible after passing PLAB 1, ideally within the first month of receiving your result. Do not wait until you feel fully prepared before booking; book a slot and prepare in parallel, as slots can become scarce.

If your PLAB 1 pass is approaching its two-year expiry and you have not yet sat PLAB 2, contact the GMC immediately. There is limited provision for extensions in exceptional circumstances, but the two-year window is strictly enforced in most cases. Missing it means resitting PLAB 1 from scratch.

AKT Validity Window and Its Implications

A critical planning factor for all candidates is the validity window of the AKT. Once you pass the AKT, your result is valid for three years (36 months). You must pass the CPSA within this three-year window. If you do not sit or pass the CPSA before the AKT validity expires, your AKT pass is voided and you must resit the AKT before attempting the CPSA again.

This three-year window gives most candidates ample time, but it can become a pressure point if you face multiple CPSA resits, career changes, health issues, or periods abroad. Plan your CPSA attempts proactively within the first 18 to 24 months after passing the AKT, leaving room for at least one resit without running into the validity boundary.

For IMGs, the equivalent PLAB validity window is: PLAB 2 must be sat within two years of passing PLAB 1. The shift to UKMLA nomenclature (AKT/CPSA) brings these validity windows in line for UK students and IMGs on the unified pathway.

What Happens If You Miss the Booking Window

Missing a booking window means waiting for the next sitting — typically three months away. This is not just a minor inconvenience; for IMGs, it can affect visa timing, employment offers, and the validity of other documents (such as an English language test result that is approaching its two-year expiry date). For UK medical students, a missed sitting could delay graduation or push you into a late FPAS application cycle, potentially affecting your foundation post allocation.

There is no mechanism to add yourself to a waiting list for a full sitting, though the GMC occasionally releases additional seats closer to the exam date due to cancellations. Checking the booking portal regularly in the weeks before an exam is worth doing if you missed the initial booking window.

If you miss a window due to a genuine emergency or medical issue, submit a mitigating circumstances application to the GMC (for IMGs) or your medical school (for UK students) as soon as possible. The earlier you submit, the more options remain available to you.

Result Release Dates

PLAB 1 / AKT results are typically released within six weeks of the sitting date. The GMC notifies candidates by email when results are available via GMC Online. Results are not released on a fixed calendar date per sitting — the turnaround can vary between four and eight weeks depending on the sitting and any marking review processes.

PLAB 2 / CPSA results take longer due to the complexity of marking OSCE assessments. Expect to wait eight to twelve weeks after your sitting date for PLAB 2 results. As with PLAB 1, results are released via GMC Online and accompanied by a detailed feedback report if you fail (which is invaluable for resit preparation).

FPAS Application Dates and Their Relationship to UKMLA

For UK medical students, the Foundation Programme Application System (FPAS) timetable runs in parallel with UKMLA examination windows. Here is how the two interact:

  • AKT results feed into the Educational Performance Measure (EPM), which is part of the FPAS application score.
  • FPAS applications typically open in October/November of the final year of medical school.
  • Shortlisting and preferences are submitted in December/January.
  • Allocation outcomes are released in March.
  • If your AKT result is not yet available when FPAS applications open, your EPM will be calculated from available data and may be updated when the result is received — check with your school and FPAS about how this is handled in your cohort year.

International Test Centre Dates and Availability

PLAB 1 is available at international centres in multiple countries, though availability and the number of seats per centre varies considerably. Countries with international PLAB 1 centres include India (multiple cities), Nigeria (Lagos and Abuja), Pakistan (Islamabad and Karachi), Egypt (historically Alexandria, though this is under review), Australia, Canada, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka.

The GMC has announced reductions in international centre availability due to declining demand — centres in Dhaka, Alexandria, Accra, and Chennai are being closed from 2027 onwards. If you were planning to sit in one of these locations, check the latest GMC guidance and adjust your plans accordingly.

Candidates in countries without an international centre must travel to sit. Some candidates from Africa or Central Asia travel to the UK specifically to sit PLAB 1, combining the exam trip with a familiarisation visit if they plan to work in the UK. Factor travel costs and visa requirements into your overall UKMLA planning budget and timeline.

Frequently Asked Questions About UKMLA Key Dates

How far in advance should I start planning my UKMLA journey as an IMG?

You should start planning at least 18 to 24 months before you want to pass the final component of the UKMLA. This allows time for MyIntealth verification (up to 6 months in some cases), English language testing and potential retakes, exam booking and preparation, and one resit if needed without pressure. Candidates who start 12 months before their target date often find themselves rushed, especially if verification or English language testing takes longer than expected.

Can I sit PLAB 1 outside the UK?

Yes. PLAB 1 is available at international test centres in multiple countries including India, Nigeria, Pakistan, Egypt (subject to centre availability), Australia, Canada, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka. International sittings run on the same quarterly schedule as UK sittings. Note that not all countries have the same number of available seats per sitting — popular centres fill very quickly. Check the GMC website and your GMC Online account for the current list of active international centres, as some locations are being phased out from 2027.

When are PLAB 1 results released?

PLAB 1 results are typically released within four to six weeks of the sitting date, though the GMC does not publish an exact results release date in advance. You will receive an email notification when your result is available to view in GMC Online. Do not make major travel or accommodation bookings contingent on receiving a result by a specific date — build in flexibility for potential delays in the results release.

What happens if I miss the two-year window to sit PLAB 2 after passing PLAB 1?

If you do not sit PLAB 2 within two years of your PLAB 1 pass date, your PLAB 1 result expires. You will need to resit PLAB 1 before being eligible to attempt PLAB 2. This two-year window is enforced strictly, and extensions are only granted in exceptional circumstances (such as serious illness) supported by appropriate evidence. If you are approaching the expiry date and have not yet sat PLAB 2, contact the GMC immediately to discuss your options. Booking PLAB 2 as early as possible after your PLAB 1 pass — even before you feel fully prepared — is strongly advisable given the limited availability of PLAB 2 slots.

Do resit attempts affect my FPAS application as a UK medical student?

Your AKT result contributes to your FPAS Educational Performance Measure (EPM). If you resit the AKT, it is your most recent result that is used for EPM calculation in most circumstances — check the current FPAS guidance for the exact rules in your application year, as these can change. A lower AKT score from a resit could affect your EPM and therefore your foundation programme allocation. However, passing is always more important than maximising the score on the first attempt. Discuss the implications with your medical school's careers team before your FPAS application window opens.

Conclusion

The UKMLA key dates framework is one of the most important practical aspects of medical licensing in the UK. Whether you are a UK medical student managing your AKT and FPAS timing or an IMG navigating international test centres, verification timelines, and PLAB 2 booking queues, the candidates who succeed are almost always the ones who plan the furthest in advance. Use the backwards-planning table in this guide to map your personal timeline, set calendar reminders for every booking window, and build in buffer time wherever possible.
