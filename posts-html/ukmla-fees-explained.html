UKMLA fees are one of the first things every aspiring UK-licensed doctor needs to understand before beginning the registration journey. Whether you are a UK medical student whose costs are covered by your institution, or an International Medical Graduate (IMG) paying entirely out of pocket, knowing the exact charges — and the hidden costs that sit alongside them — is essential for effective financial planning. This guide breaks down every fee associated with the UKMLA process in 2026, explains the refund and cancellation rules, compares costs across popular currencies, and offers practical strategies for reducing the overall financial burden of gaining GMC registration.

The total cost of the UKMLA pathway for an IMG can exceed £1,800 before a single day of clinical practice in the UK. That figure rises considerably once you factor in study materials, living expenses during exam preparation, travel to Manchester for the CPSA, and the annual retention fee payable after registration. Understanding where every pound goes — and which costs are avoidable — is the difference between a financial plan that works and one that leaves candidates stranded mid-journey.

In this comprehensive guide we cover the 2026 fee schedule in full, the refund and cancellation policy, currency conversions for candidates from India, Nigeria, the UAE and the United States, financial hardship options, and a detailed comparison of how UKMLA costs stack up against other international medical licensing exams.

How Much Are the UKMLA Fees in 2026?

The UKMLA pathway involves several distinct fees payable to the GMC and, for IMGs, to the Pearson VUE testing network. Each charge is separate, non-bundled, and payable at different stages of the process. As of 2026 the headline fees are: £283 for the Applied Knowledge Test (AKT), also known as PLAB 1 for IMGs; £1,036 for the Clinical and Professional Skills Assessment (CPSA), also known as PLAB 2 for IMGs; and £481 for the initial GMC registration fee once both assessments are passed. Together these three core charges total approximately £1,800 for an IMG completing the full pathway from first exam attempt to GMC registration.

The GMC reviews its fee schedule periodically, and the most recent formal revision took effect in April 2026 following the publication of the General Medical Council Registration Fees Regulations 2026. Candidates should always verify current charges directly on the GMC website before booking, as fees are subject to change with notice. The table below shows the main fee categories at a glance.

UKMLA Fees for UK Medical Students

For UK medical students enrolled at a GMC-approved UK medical school, the UKMLA fees structure is fundamentally different from that facing IMGs. UK medical students do not pay the AKT or CPSA exam fees directly. Instead, the GMC charges these fees to the medical school, which incorporates the cost into its overall programme delivery. This means the cost is effectively included within the tuition fees students pay to their university rather than appearing as a separate, upfront charge.

This arrangement was a deliberate policy decision by the GMC when designing the UKMLA framework. The logic is straightforward: the UKMLA replaces the historic summative assessments that medical schools already ran as part of their degree programmes. Because universities were already funding those final-year exams, transitioning to the UKMLA did not create an additional financial burden for individual students — the cost simply shifted from an internal assessment to an external, GMC-administered one, with the medical school absorbing the invoice.

Practically, this means UK medical students receive an invitation letter from their medical school to book both the AKT and CPSA. They log in to the relevant booking portal, select dates, and confirm attendance without entering any payment details. No money changes hands directly between the student and the GMC at the exam stage. The financial consideration for UK graduates arises later, when they apply for provisional GMC registration upon graduation. At that point, a registration application fee of approximately £165 is payable, significantly lower than the full £481 fee charged to IMGs because UK graduates receive provisional rather than full registration initially.

UK graduates are therefore in a significantly more financially advantageous position than their IMG counterparts — the two largest costs (AKT and CPSA exam fees) are covered, leaving only the relatively modest registration application and the annual retention fee of £431 payable each January to maintain registration.

UKMLA Fees for International Medical Graduates (IMGs)

For IMGs, the UKMLA fees landscape is more complex and substantially more expensive. IMGs must pay for each component of the assessment individually, and these payments are non-negotiable, non-income-tested, and non-refundable except under specific cancellation circumstances. Understanding each element is critical before committing to the pathway.

Applied Knowledge Test (AKT / PLAB 1) Fee — £283

The AKT, which IMGs access through the PLAB 1 route, costs £283 per attempt in 2026. This fee is payable through the GMC Online portal and must be settled at the point of booking. IMGs can sit the AKT at Pearson VUE test centres worldwide, which means candidates in India, Nigeria, Pakistan, Egypt, and other high-volume IMG countries can take the exam without travelling to the UK. The booking window typically opens several months in advance, and popular test centre dates fill quickly, particularly in major cities like Mumbai, Lagos, Karachi, and Cairo.

The £283 fee covers a single attempt only. There is no cap on the number of attempts an IMG can make at the AKT, but each re-sit requires a fresh payment of £283. Candidates who fail the AKT should factor re-sit costs into their financial planning from the outset. Historically, a significant proportion of IMG candidates require more than one attempt, particularly in the wake of the MLA content map update that came into effect in August 2024 and introduced new topics including transgender health, genetics, social determinants of health, and expanded women's health presentations.

Clinical and Professional Skills Assessment (CPSA / PLAB 2) Fee — £1,036

The CPSA, accessed by IMGs through the PLAB 2 route, is by far the most expensive single element of the UKMLA pathway at £1,036 per attempt. This fee is substantially higher than the AKT fee because it covers the costs of a full OSCE circuit administered at the GMC's Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester, including trained simulated patients, trained examiners, marking, logistics, and the infrastructure of an in-person clinical exam.

Unlike the AKT, the CPSA cannot be sat overseas — all PLAB 2 / CPSA sittings take place in Manchester. This adds a layer of additional cost for IMGs who must travel from abroad or from other parts of the UK. Many international candidates combine their CPSA booking with a period of preparation in the UK, which itself carries accommodation and subsistence costs on top of the exam fee. For a candidate travelling from India for a two-week CPSA preparation and exam trip, the total spend including flights, accommodation, food, and the exam fee can easily reach £2,000–£2,500 for this component alone.

The number of CPSA re-sits is not capped, but each costs £1,036. Refugee doctors receive a concessionary rate: the first two PLAB 2 attempts are charged at half price (approximately £518 each), with full fees applying from the third attempt onward.

GMC Registration Fee — £481

Once an IMG has passed both the AKT and CPSA, confirmed English language proficiency (IELTS Academic overall 7.5 with no component below 7.0, or OET minimum Grade B in all four sub-tests), and completed the required internship experience, they can apply for full GMC registration. The initial registration fee is £481. This is a one-time payment for the initial application; once registered, the annual retention fee of £431 applies every January to maintain active registration.

Hidden Costs Beyond the UKMLA Exam Fees

The headline fees for the AKT, CPSA, and GMC registration represent only part of the true financial commitment of the UKMLA pathway. A thorough budget must account for a range of additional costs that are easy to overlook during initial planning but can amount to several thousand pounds.

  • English language testing: IELTS Academic costs approximately £180–£200 per attempt in most countries. OET costs approximately £500 per attempt. Candidates who do not achieve the required scores on the first attempt face repeat fees.
  • Study materials and question banks: Quality UKMLA AKT question banks cost between £40 and £200 for subscription access. Many candidates use multiple resources, bringing this total to £300–£500 over a preparation period of six to twelve months.
  • CPSA preparation courses: In-person OSCE preparation courses in the UK typically cost £500–£1,500 for a multi-day intensive. Online OSCE preparation packages range from £100 to £400.
  • Travel to Manchester for CPSA: Return flights from South Asia typically cost £500–£900. Return flights from West Africa range from £600–£1,000. Domestic UK travel (if preparing elsewhere) is lower but still significant.
  • Accommodation during CPSA: Hotels in Manchester near the GMC Clinical Assessment Centre range from £60–£150 per night. A two-week preparation and exam period can cost £840–£2,100 in accommodation alone.
  • Document verification: Some primary qualifications require verification through the Education Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG) or equivalent national bodies, which carry their own fees.
  • Annual retention: After registration, the £431 annual retention fee is payable every January for as long as you remain on the register. Over a 40-year career, this amounts to more than £17,000.

UKMLA Fees Compared to Other Countries' Licensing Exams

Understanding how UKMLA fees compare to equivalent licensing pathways in other English-speaking countries helps IMGs assess the relative value of pursuing UK registration versus alternatives such as the USMLE (USA), AMC (Australia), or MCCQE (Canada).

The United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE) involves three separate steps with fees of approximately $985 (Step 1), $985 (Step 2 CK), and $1,295 (Step 3), plus an Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG) certification fee of approximately $200. Total pathway costs for an IMG exceed $3,500, not including travel to the US for clinical rotations that may be required. The Australian Medical Council (AMC) pathway involves a multiple-choice exam fee of approximately AUD 2,600 and an OSCE fee of approximately AUD 5,600, totalling over AUD 8,000 (approximately £4,000 GBP) before registration fees. The Medical Council of Canada's pathway involves fees in the range of CAD 3,000–4,500 across its examination components.

By comparison, the UKMLA pathway at approximately £1,800 for core exam and registration fees (assuming first-attempt passes) represents relatively good value among high-income country medical licensing systems, particularly given that the CPSA is administered in a world-class facility with trained simulated patients and that GMC registration opens access to one of the most internationally respected medical registers in the world.

How to Pay UKMLA Fees and the Refund Policy

All UKMLA-related fees are paid through your GMC Online account. To make a payment, log in to your GMC Online profile, navigate to the relevant section (PLAB booking for AKT or CPSA, or registration application for the registration fee), and pay by debit or credit card. The GMC accepts Visa, Mastercard, and most major cards. Payment is required in full at the time of booking; instalment plans are not available.

The GMC operates a tiered cancellation and refund policy for the AKT and CPSA. The amount refunded depends on how far in advance of your exam date you cancel:

  • More than 42 calendar days before the exam: Refund of the full fee minus a 10% administration/cancellation charge.
  • Between 14 and 42 calendar days before the exam: No refund is available under standard circumstances.
  • Fewer than 14 calendar days before the exam: No refund and no deferral; the full fee is forfeited.

The GMC may exercise discretion and refund cancellation fees in exceptional circumstances. Accepted circumstances typically include: visa refusal or visa processing delays that prevent travel to the test centre; introduction of government travel restrictions (such as those imposed during the COVID-19 pandemic); serious ill health supported by a medical certificate; or bereavement of an immediate family member. If you believe your circumstances qualify, you must cancel your booking through the portal first and then submit evidence by email to the GMC within a reasonable timeframe. The GMC will assess the evidence and make a decision; this process can take several weeks.

For GMC registration fees, the situation is different. Once paid, the registration application fee is generally non-refundable regardless of outcome, including if the application is refused on grounds of fitness to practise or inadequate documentation. Candidates should ensure all documents are in order before submitting a registration application.

How to Budget and Reduce Your UKMLA Costs

Given the significant total financial outlay involved in the UKMLA pathway, systematic budgeting and cost-reduction strategies can make a meaningful difference, particularly for candidates from lower-income countries where the exchange rate amplifies the pound cost considerably.

Prepare Thoroughly Before Your First Attempt

The single most impactful cost-reduction strategy is passing both the AKT and CPSA on your first attempt. Each re-sit costs £283 (AKT) or £1,036 (CPSA). A candidate who uses a single additional attempt at both assessments adds £1,319 to their costs — more than the price of many comprehensive preparation courses. Investing in quality preparation materials upfront is almost always cheaper than funding re-sits. Use GMC-approved question banks, work through the full MLA content map systematically, and complete multiple timed mock papers before your real sitting.

Book Test Centres in Lower-Cost Locations

For the AKT, Pearson VUE has test centres in over 180 countries. Candidates who live near a border with a neighbouring country where test centres are less busy or where international travel is cheaper may find it worthwhile to travel to a nearer, less-congested centre. Book as early as possible to secure your preferred date and location; last-minute bookings often face limited availability, which can push candidates toward more expensive or inconvenient centres.

Plan Your Manchester Trip Efficiently

For the CPSA, advance planning of travel and accommodation significantly reduces costs. Booking flights six to twelve weeks in advance and using budget airlines where available can reduce travel costs substantially. Sharing accommodation with other CPSA candidates (many preparation forums and social media groups facilitate this) can halve hotel costs. Choosing accommodation slightly further from the city centre with good public transport links to the GMC centre is often significantly cheaper than city-centre hotels.

Financial Aid and Hardship Concessions

The GMC offers specific fee concessions for refugee doctors through its refugee doctor assistance programme. Under this scheme, refugee doctors can take the first two PLAB 1 (AKT) sittings free of charge, and the first two PLAB 2 (CPSA) sittings at 50% of the standard fee. To qualify, doctors must hold refugee status or have a pending asylum application recognised by the relevant national authority. Evidence of status must be provided to the GMC before the concession is applied. There is currently no general income-based hardship fund for non-refugee IMGs, meaning most candidates must fund the pathway from personal savings, family support, or, in some cases, bank loans.

Timeline Your Pathway to Avoid Overlap

Each component of the UKMLA pathway has a validity period. AKT results are valid for five years. There is therefore no financial pressure to rush into the CPSA immediately after passing the AKT if your personal circumstances make it advantageous to delay. Similarly, English language test results (IELTS or OET) have a defined validity period of two years, so timing your language test to align with your overall pathway timeline avoids the cost of retesting.

UKMLA Fee History and Changes Over Time

Understanding how UKMLA-related fees have evolved over time gives context to current charges and helps with forward financial planning. PLAB fees have increased substantially over the past decade. In 2015, PLAB 1 cost approximately £225 and PLAB 2 approximately £665. By 2020, those figures had risen to approximately £239 (PLAB 1) and £869 (PLAB 2). The 2024 alignment of PLAB with the MLA standard coincided with further fee increases, bringing the charges to their current 2026 levels of £283 and £1,036 respectively. The GMC attributes fee increases primarily to rising operational costs, investment in digital infrastructure, and the costs of standard-setting and quality assurance for the new MLA framework.

The introduction of the MLA framework did not reduce fees for any cohort of candidates. Some stakeholders argued that IMGs should see a fee reduction given that the MLA content map was being applied to existing PLAB infrastructure rather than a wholly new exam; however, the GMC has maintained that the enhanced standard-setting processes, expanded content, and quality assurance measures associated with MLA compliance justify the current fee levels.

Frequently Asked Questions About UKMLA Fees

Do UK medical students pay any UKMLA fees directly?

No. UK medical students enrolled at GMC-approved medical schools do not pay for the AKT or CPSA directly. The fees are invoiced to the medical school and absorbed within the overall cost of the medical degree. Students receive booking invitations through their institution and complete the process without making any direct payment to the GMC for the assessments themselves. The only fees payable directly by UK medical graduates are the registration application fee (approximately £165 for provisional registration) and the annual retention fee (£431) once on the register.

Is the UKMLA fee refundable if I fail?

No. Exam fees are not refundable on the grounds of failure. Whether you pass or fail the AKT or CPSA, the fee paid for that sitting is retained by the GMC. Refunds are only available when you cancel your booking before the exam within the permitted cancellation window (more than 42 days before the exam, subject to the 10% cancellation charge) or in demonstrable exceptional circumstances such as visa refusal or serious illness.

How many times can I sit the AKT and CPSA, and what does it cost?

There is no official cap on the number of AKT or CPSA attempts for IMGs. Each attempt requires a fresh payment at the standard rate: £283 per AKT attempt and £1,036 per CPSA attempt. There are also no mandatory waiting periods between attempts beyond the practical constraint of available exam dates. From a cost perspective, every additional attempt is a significant financial commitment, reinforcing the importance of adequate preparation before each sitting.

Are there any fee discounts for IMGs from lower-income countries?

Currently, the GMC does not operate a sliding-scale fee system based on country of origin or income level. The only formal concession available is the refugee doctor assistance scheme, which provides free AKT attempts and half-price CPSA attempts for doctors with refugee status. There is no general hardship fund or discounted rate for IMGs from low- or middle-income countries. Candidates are encouraged to save and plan accordingly, and several community organisations and diaspora networks offer informal interest-free loans or financial support for medical licensing.

When exactly do I pay each fee — all upfront or separately?

Fees are payable separately at each stage of the process. You pay the AKT fee when you book your AKT sitting. You pay the CPSA fee when you book your CPSA sitting (which can only be done after passing the AKT). You pay the GMC registration fee when you submit your full registration application (after passing both assessments and meeting all other eligibility requirements). There is no requirement to pay all fees upfront, and no deposit system for holding a place before full payment is required.

What happens to my fee if the GMC cancels my exam?

If the GMC cancels an exam session for reasons within its control (administrative error, venue problem, safety concerns), candidates are entitled to a full refund of the exam fee or the option to transfer their booking to an alternative date at no additional charge. The GMC has historically handled such situations promptly. If an exam is cancelled due to exceptional external circumstances beyond the GMC's control (such as a national emergency or infrastructure failure), the GMC will consider cases individually and aim to minimise financial detriment to candidates.

Does the annual GMC retention fee increase each year?

The annual retention fee (currently £431) is reviewed periodically by the GMC and has increased at various points over the past decade. The GMC must consult publicly before changing retention fees and typically provides at least six months' notice of any increase. Fee changes are announced on the GMC website and in direct communications to registered doctors. Increases have historically been in the range of £10–£30 per year when they occur, but not every year sees an increase.

Conclusion

Understanding UKMLA fees in full — not just the headline exam costs but the hidden travel, preparation, language testing, and annual retention charges — is the foundation of effective financial planning for the UK medical licensing journey. For UK medical students, the pathway is largely free at the point of assessment, with costs absorbed by medical schools. For IMGs, the total outlay from first AKT attempt to GMC registration typically ranges from £1,800 (best case, first attempts only) to £3,500 or more when travel, accommodation, preparation materials, and English language testing are included.

The most effective strategies for managing UKMLA costs are thorough preparation to maximise first-attempt pass rates, early booking to secure cost-effective travel and accommodation for the CPSA in Manchester, and careful timing of the various pathway components to avoid unnecessary repetition of English language or medical knowledge tests. The fees are substantial, but they represent a one-time investment in access to one of the world's most respected medical registers and a healthcare system that offers strong career development, structured training, and competitive remuneration.
