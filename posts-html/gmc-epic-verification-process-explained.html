What GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification actually is

GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification is a mandatory check that confirms your primary medical qualification, and in some cases your postgraduate or specialist qualifications, were genuinely awarded by the institution named on your certificate. According to the GMC's own guidance on primary source verification for international medical graduates, this requirement applies to any doctor who qualified at a medical school outside the UK and does not already hold provisional registration. Verification is carried out by ECFMG, a division of Intealth, through their online system, historically known as the Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials, or EPIC, and now delivered through the MyIntealth platform.

The mechanics are straightforward in principle: ECFMG does not simply take your word, or even a certified copy of your degree, at face value. Instead, it contacts your medical school's own administration directly and asks them to confirm, through their own channels, that the degree, transcript or registration certificate you have submitted is authentic and that you are the person named on it. This is why the GMC and ECFMG both describe it as "primary source" verification: the source being checked is the institution itself, not a document you provide.

Why the GMC requires EPIC/MyIntealth verification

The GMC requires this step because it has no independent way to confirm that a degree certificate from a medical school overseas is genuine, and fraudulent or altered qualifications are a real patient safety risk if they slip through. Rather than building its own verification infrastructure across thousands of medical schools worldwide, the GMC relies on ECFMG, an organisation with, according to ECFMG's own site, close to seventy years of experience evaluating physician credentials and relationships with more than 2,500 medical schools across nearly 180 countries or territories. This arrangement means every applicant, regardless of country, goes through the same rigorous check rather than a country-specific or informal process that could vary in reliability.

It is worth being clear about what verification does and does not confirm. A successfully verified qualification tells the GMC that your degree is real and was genuinely awarded to you. It does not, by itself, mean your qualification meets the GMC's criteria for an acceptable primary medical qualification for GMC registration, nor does it replace the other requirements in that broader process, such as English language evidence, an accepted internship, and passing the UKMLA-aligned AKT and CPSA assessments (or holding a recognised exemption). Verification is one necessary condition among several, not a substitute for the rest of the registration pathway.

EPIC and MyIntealth: same service, two names

A recurring point of confusion is whether EPIC and MyIntealth are the same thing or two different systems. Technically, they are not identical: MyIntealth is the broader online environment operated by Intealth for accessing any of its services, including those from its divisions ECFMG and FAIMER, while EPIC is the specific primary source verification service you access from within your MyIntealth account. In practice, however, almost everyone, including many candidates, forums, and even some GMC-facing paperwork, still refers to the whole process simply as "EPIC verification," because that was the name candidates used for years before the MyIntealth platform launched. This guide uses both names together throughout for exactly that reason: whichever term you encounter, the underlying requirement and process described here is the same.

Step-by-step: how the GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification process works

The GMC's own guidance breaks the process down into a clear sequence, and understanding each stage in advance helps you avoid the common trap of assuming the process happens faster, or more automatically, than it actually does.

Step 1: Set up your MyIntealth account and confirm your identity

The process begins by creating an online MyIntealth account, which includes confirming your identity with ECFMG. According to ECFMG's own EPIC overview on the official ECFMG/Intealth website, a MyIntealth account is required to apply for any of Intealth's services, and once your account is fully established, meaning you have a valid identification form on file, you are issued a MyIntealth ID. This ID becomes an important reference point later, since you will need to provide it when you submit your GMC registration application.

Step 2: Establish an EPIC Portfolio within your MyIntealth account

Once your MyIntealth account and identity verification are in place, you log in and select EPIC under the Service tab to establish your EPIC Portfolio. This portfolio is effectively your secure, digital record of verified credentials, and it is designed to be reusable: once a credential is verified, you can request that ECFMG send verification reports to other organisations in future, not just the GMC, without repeating the whole process from scratch.

Step 3: Upload the qualifications that need to be verified

With your EPIC Portfolio established, you upload the specific qualifications that require verification. As you upload each document, you select the GMC as the intended recipient of the EPIC report. ECFMG's guidance lists the credential types physicians can typically upload, including the final medical diploma, an alternate graduation document, the final medical school transcript, a pregraduate internship certificate, postgraduate training credentials, specialist qualifications, a certificate of good standing, and a medical registration certificate or licence to practise. If any uploaded document is not in English, you must also provide an English translation that meets ECFMG's translation requirements.

Step 4: ECFMG verifies your qualification with your awarding institution

This is the stage where ECFMG contacts your medical school (or other issuing institution) directly to confirm the qualification is genuine. According to the GMC's guidance on what happens during the verification process, ECFMG will email you when they send your qualification for verification, and again once it has been verified. You can monitor progress at any point by logging into your EPIC account; the GMC itself cannot provide updates on the status of an ECFMG verification, since the process is managed entirely by ECFMG.

Step 5: Submit your GMC application with your MyIntealth ID

Once your qualification is verified, or even once ECFMG has sent it for verification, you can move forward with your GMC registration application, providing your MyIntealth ID as part of that submission. If you previously used the older EPIC system before the MyIntealth platform existed, you may also hold a separate EPIC ID, which some GMC-facing paperwork still references alongside the newer MyIntealth ID.

The GMC's own guidance is explicit on sequencing: "You can apply for registration once ECFMG have sent your qualification to be verified by your institution but, if you can, we recommend that you have your qualification(s) verified before applying." In other words, starting early is not just a nice-to-have, it materially changes how smoothly your registration application proceeds.

Certificate issuance: what happens once your qualification is verified

Certificate issuance, in the context of EPIC/MyIntealth verification, does not mean ECFMG issues you a brand new certificate. Rather, once your uploaded qualification has been successfully checked against your medical school's own records, ECFMG marks that credential as verified within your EPIC Portfolio and generates a verification report, sometimes still referred to informally as an "EPIC certificate" by candidates, that can be sent to any organisation you authorise, including the GMC. Understanding this distinction matters because some candidates mistakenly expect a physical or emailed certificate similar to their original degree parchment; instead, what you actually receive is confirmation, delivered securely through the MyIntealth platform, that your credential has passed primary source verification.

According to ECFMG's own description of how EPIC works, verification reports include your name, photo, date of birth, MyIntealth ID (and EPIC ID if one was previously issued), your name exactly as it appears on the credential, and details of the institution that issued it, alongside a copy of the credential itself and, where relevant, its English translation. The GMC receives this report through a secure channel rather than as an email attachment you forward yourself, which is part of why the process is considered more robust than simply submitting scanned documents directly to the GMC.

One practical implication follows directly from this: because the report is tied to your EPIC Portfolio rather than a one-off submission, once a credential has been verified you do not need to repeat the entire verification process if you later apply to a different organisation, whether that is a different country's medical regulator, an employer, or a training body, provided you request a fresh report be shared and, if more than 12 months have passed since verification, ask ECFMG to renew the permission to share it.

How EPIC/MyIntealth verification interacts with provisional, full and specialist registration

The specific documents ECFMG needs to verify can differ depending on which category of GMC registration you are applying for. Doctors applying for provisional registration, typically because their internship is not yet complete or confirmed, generally need their primary medical qualification verified at minimum, since provisional registration exists specifically to allow supervised practice before an internship is finished. Doctors applying for full registration usually need their primary medical qualification and evidence of a complete, accepted internship verified together. Doctors applying through the specialist or GP portfolio route may additionally need postgraduate training credentials or specialist qualification certificates verified, since that route depends on evidencing equivalence to a UK-trained specialist or GP. Our types of GMC registration explained guide covers these categories in far more depth, and is worth reading before you decide exactly which documents to submit for verification, since submitting the wrong set can mean paying ECFMG's non-refundable fees twice.

Why UK medical graduates do not go through EPIC/MyIntealth verification

A question that comes up often, particularly from candidates comparing notes with UK-trained peers, is why UK medical graduates never mention EPIC or MyIntealth at all. The answer is straightforward: the GMC's requirement for independent primary source verification applies specifically to doctors who qualified at a medical school outside the UK. UK medical schools are quality-assured directly by the GMC itself, through ongoing inspection and accreditation of their curricula, assessment methods and standards, including the Medical Licensing Assessment framework that both UK final-year students and IMGs are now aligned against. Because the GMC already has a direct, ongoing quality assurance relationship with every UK medical school, it does not need a third party to independently confirm that a UK degree is genuine in the same way it does for an overseas qualification it has no comparable oversight of.

This distinction is worth understanding clearly, because it explains why the registration paperwork and timeline genuinely differ between a UK graduate and an IMG, even though both are ultimately assessed against the same Medical Licensing Assessment standard. A UK final-year student moves from finishing their degree into provisional registration relatively quickly, without an equivalent to the EPIC/MyIntealth stage, because their qualification's authenticity was never in question in the same administrative sense. An IMG, by contrast, must build in the EPIC/MyIntealth timeline as a distinct, often lengthy stage of their own registration journey, which is precisely why this guide exists as a dedicated explainer rather than being folded into a general registration overview.

Timeline factors: what actually determines how long GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification takes

There is no single official number of weeks for GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification, and any guide claiming an exact figure is oversimplifying a genuinely variable process. The GMC's own guidance is candid about this: verification time "depends on a number of factors," and the realistic range candidates should plan around is commonly weeks to a few months, heavily dependent on your medical school's administrative responsiveness, rather than a fixed number you can rely on for planning a resignation date or a visa application.

The two factors the GMC itself names

According to the GMC's guidance on what happens during the verification process, two factors principally determine how long verification takes: the time it takes for your awarding institution to respond to ECFMG, noting that most schools respond within 90 days, and whether your awarding institution accepts these requests electronically, since requests sent by post or courier take noticeably longer. Because of these two variables, the GMC "strongly recommends" starting the verification process as early as possible and, if feasible, having your qualification fully verified before you apply for registration at all.

The 90-day application window and the 12-month report validity

Two specific numbers from the GMC's own guidance are worth internalising, since they directly affect your planning. First, the GMC can only keep a registration application open for 90 days, which means if verification is still outstanding when that window closes, your application may need to be restarted. Second, once ECFMG has verified your qualification, the permission to share that report with the GMC expires if you do not apply for registration within 12 months of the verification being completed; after that point, you would need to ask ECFMG to share the report again. Neither of these numbers is a verification turnaround time in itself, but both shape how you should sequence your own application relative to when verification finishes.

Other realistic contributors to delay

Beyond the two factors the GMC names directly, several other practical issues commonly extend real-world timelines: administrative staff turnover at your medical school between when you graduated and when you request verification, incomplete or incorrectly formatted document uploads that need resubmission, translation requirements not being met on the first attempt, and simply the volume of requests a given institution's registrar's office is processing at any one time. None of these are things ECFMG or the GMC can resolve on your behalf, which is precisely why starting early and following up proactively matters more here than in almost any other stage of the registration process. How the process differs by country and medical school responsiveness

The underlying EPIC/MyIntealth process itself, account creation, portfolio setup, document upload, primary source checking and certificate issuance, is identical everywhere in the world; ECFMG runs one global system, not a different one per country. What genuinely differs is how quickly individual medical schools respond, whether they can handle requests electronically, and how well-organised their alumni or registrar's records are. This is why candidates from the same country can have wildly different experiences depending on which specific medical school they attended, and why generic country-level advice should always be read as a pattern, not a guarantee.

India

India is the single largest source country for IMGs pursuing UK registration, and verification experiences vary considerably across its large number of medical colleges, some of which respond to ECFMG requests quickly and electronically, while others are slower or still rely on postal correspondence. Because of the sheer number of Indian candidates and the specific documents involved, such as State Medical Council or National Medical Commission registration certificates, this topic deserves its own dedicated treatment. Readers who qualified in India should treat this article as the general framework and go to the deeper India-specific walkthrough for a full breakdown of account setup, document specifics and what Indian candidates commonly experience.

Nigeria

Nigerian medical graduates follow the same EPIC/MyIntealth process, with verification requests going to their awarding medical school and, in some cases, to the Medical and Dental Council of Nigeria for registration confirmation. As with any country, responsiveness varies by institution, and Nigerian candidates planning their route to GMC registration should read our dedicated UKMLA for Nigerian doctors guide for country-specific eligibility, fee and process detail alongside this general verification explainer.

Pakistan

Pakistani doctors similarly go through the same underlying verification steps, with their medical school and the Pakistan Medical and Dental Council typically involved in confirming credentials. Response times again depend on the specific institution rather than the country as a whole. For the fuller picture of eligibility, fees and the registration route specific to Pakistani candidates, see our UKMLA for Pakistani doctors guide.

Other countries

Candidates from Bangladesh, Egypt, the Gulf states, Sri Lanka, South Africa, Kenya and other East African countries, Jamaica and Trinidad, Jordan and Iraq, China, the Philippines, Vietnam and Indonesia, and elsewhere all go through the identical EPIC/MyIntealth process described in this guide. ECFMG's own site notes it maintains relationships with more than 2,500 medical schools across close to 180 countries or territories, so wherever you trained, the same primary-source verification principle applies: your school is contacted directly, and the response time is a function of that specific institution's administrative capacity, not a fixed national average. If your country is not covered by a dedicated post yet, this general guide remains your most reliable starting point, alongside the GMC's own registration and licensing pages.

Fees: EPIC/MyIntealth costs are separate from GMC fees

A detail that catches many candidates off guard is that ECFMG's verification fees are entirely separate from, and in addition to, GMC registration and exam fees. According to the GMC's guidance on how much it costs to verify your qualification, the cost varies depending on how many qualifications you submit, fees are set by ECFMG rather than the GMC, and they are charged in US dollars regardless of which country you are applying from, so you should check ECFMG's own fee schedule and current exchange rates before budgeting rather than assuming a GBP figure. Refunds are not available once ECFMG has started the verification process, so it is worth being certain of exactly which documents you need verified before submitting.

One notable exception: doctors who have been granted refugee status in the UK can have their EPIC/MyIntealth verification fees paid by the GMC directly, rather than through ECFMG's standard fee route, though the GMC advises contacting them before creating a MyIntealth account if this applies to you. An illustrative scenario: how a verification timeline can actually unfold

Because ranges and hedged figures can feel abstract, it helps to walk through a realistic, illustrative scenario of how EPIC/MyIntealth verification might unfold for a typical candidate, while remembering that your own experience will depend on the specific factors covered above. This example is illustrative only, not a guarantee of any particular timeline, but it demonstrates how the individual stages tend to stack up in practice.

Suppose a candidate decides in January to begin their route to GMC registration. In week one, they create a MyIntealth account and complete identity verification, which itself can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks depending on how quickly supporting identity documents are accepted. By early February, they have established their EPIC Portfolio and uploaded their final medical diploma, transcript and internship certificate, selecting the GMC as the intended recipient of the eventual report. ECFMG then sends the verification request to the candidate's medical school in mid-February.

From this point, the timeline genuinely depends on the institution. If the medical school responds electronically and efficiently, the candidate might see their qualification marked as verified within four to eight weeks, putting verification substantially complete by around April. If, instead, the school primarily handles correspondence by post, requires the request to be re-routed internally to a different department, or is simply slow to prioritise third-party verification requests, the same process could easily stretch to four, five or six months, pushing completion into the summer. Neither outcome is unusual, and both fall within the "weeks to a few months" range this guide has emphasised throughout, precisely because the medical school's own administrative capacity, not the ECFMG process itself, is the variable driving the difference.

Meanwhile, in either scenario, the candidate would typically be progressing other strands of their registration journey in parallel: sitting an English language test, preparing for the AKT, and gathering supporting registration documents from their home country's medical regulator. Because the GMC's 90-day application window only starts once you actually submit your application, a sensible candidate in this scenario would wait until verification is either complete, or has at least been sent to the institution with a reasonable expectation of a timely response, before submitting their GMC application, so the clock does not run out while they are still waiting on their medical school.

Common mistakes candidates make with EPIC/MyIntealth verification

Several avoidable mistakes recur often enough among candidates from every country that they are worth naming explicitly.

  • Starting verification too late. Because verification depends on your medical school's own response time, leaving it until after you have booked exams or arranged a visa is the single most common cause of avoidable delay.
  • Assuming EPIC and MyIntealth are separate, competing systems. They are one connected service; confusion here sometimes leads candidates to create duplicate accounts or submit documents twice unnecessarily.
  • Uploading documents in the wrong format or without a required translation. ECFMG has specific translation and formatting requirements, and submissions that do not meet them are typically returned for correction, adding weeks to the timeline.
  • Not selecting the GMC as the report recipient at upload. Because you choose the destination organisation as you upload each credential, forgetting this step means the completed report is not automatically sent where it is needed.
  • Letting the verified report's 12-month sharing window lapse. Candidates who verify early and then delay their GMC application by more than a year may need to ask ECFMG to re-share the report before applying.
  • Confusing GMC processing time with EPIC/MyIntealth verification time. These are two separate clocks; a completed verification does not mean your GMC application itself will be processed instantly.
  • Assuming a refund is available if you change your mind. ECFMG does not offer refunds once verification has started, so confirm exactly which qualifications need verifying before submitting.
  • Treating the 90-day application window as flexible. The GMC can only keep an application open for 90 days, so verification delays that run past this window can force you to restart the registration application itself.

What to do if your medical school is slow or unresponsive

If weeks turn into months with no update on your verification, the most effective first step is to check your own EPIC/MyIntealth account for the current status, since ECFMG will have logged whatever correspondence has taken place with your institution. If the delay appears to sit with your medical school rather than ECFMG, contacting your school's registrar, alumni relations office, or international office directly, and asking them specifically about an ECFMG/EPIC verification request, is usually more effective than waiting passively. Many institutions receive these requests rarely enough that a polite, direct follow-up from the graduate themselves can meaningfully speed up an otherwise stalled request.

It is also worth checking whether your institution can respond electronically rather than by post, since the GMC's own guidance specifically flags postal responses as slower. If your medical school's process only supports postal verification, ask whether an alternative contact or department can expedite an electronic response instead. Where a qualification genuinely cannot be verified within a reasonable timeframe, the GMC's guidance states it will "need to understand the reasons why before advising you on the next steps," so keep a clear record of your own follow-up attempts in case you need to explain the situation to the GMC directly.

If you are unsure whether your specific qualification is even acceptable to the GMC before you invest time and money in verification, it is worth confirming this first via the GMC's guidance on GMC registration requirements for international doctors and the pillar-level registration guide, since verifying a qualification the GMC would not accept anyway wastes both time and ECFMG's non-refundable fees.

Practical escalation steps, in order

When a verification request appears stalled, working through a structured escalation sequence tends to be more productive than repeatedly checking your EPIC account and hoping for an update. First, confirm within your own account exactly what stage the request is at and whether ECFMG has actually sent the request to your institution yet, since delays sometimes happen on ECFMG's side before the request even reaches your school. Second, identify the correct department at your medical school: many verification requests stall simply because they were routed to a general admissions office rather than the specific registrar, examinations, or alumni records office that handles third-party verification requests. Third, if you graduated some years ago, consider whether your school has undergone any administrative restructuring, merger, or system change, since older records sometimes sit with a different department or even a successor institution than the one you originally attended. Fourth, where possible, ask a contact who graduated more recently, or a current staff member you know, whether the school's verification process has a known typical turnaround, since informal local knowledge is often more accurate than assuming every institution behaves the same way.

If none of this resolves the delay after a reasonable period, and you are approaching the GMC's 90-day application window, it is entirely reasonable to contact the GMC directly to explain the situation rather than letting the application lapse silently. The GMC's own guidance acknowledges that in the rare event a qualification cannot be verified within the required timeframe, it will "need to understand the reasons why before advising you on the next steps," which implies a degree of flexibility exists for candidates who can demonstrate they have made genuine, documented efforts to resolve an institutional delay that is outside their control.

How EPIC/MyIntealth verification fits into the wider GMC registration and UKMLA timeline

EPIC/MyIntealth verification is one strand of a broader registration process that also includes English language evidence, an accepted internship where relevant, and, for most IMGs, passing the UKMLA-aligned AKT and CPSA assessments (or holding a recognised exemption). Because several of these strands can run in parallel rather than strictly one after another, most experienced candidates and advisers recommend starting verification alongside, or even before, exam preparation, precisely because it is the strand with the least predictable timeline. It is also worth remembering that registration itself is a gateway, not the final destination. Once your qualification is verified and your full application is approved, you can apply for NHS posts, but the job search and any visa sponsorship process are separate stages that begin only after registration is granted. Frequently asked questions

What is GMC EPIC verification?

GMC EPIC verification is the primary source verification process every international medical graduate must complete before GMC registration. It is run by ECFMG, a division of Intealth, through the Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials (EPIC) service, delivered via the MyIntealth platform, and confirms your medical qualifications are genuine by checking directly with the institutions that issued them.

Is EPIC the same as MyIntealth?

Not exactly, but the two names are used interchangeably. MyIntealth is the online platform where you create your account and manage everything; EPIC is the specific verification service you access within that platform. Historically candidates only ever heard the name EPIC, so many guides and even some official documents still refer to the whole process as EPIC verification.

Who runs the GMC verification process, EPIC or MyIntealth?

The verification process is managed entirely by ECFMG, a division of the not-for-profit organisation Intealth, not by the GMC itself. The GMC requires the verification as a condition of registration and receives the completed EPIC report, but all account setup, document upload, fees and communication with your medical school happen through ECFMG and MyIntealth.

How long does GMC EPIC/MyIntealth verification take?

There is no fixed timeframe. Verification commonly takes anywhere from a few weeks to several months, heavily dependent on your medical school's administrative responsiveness and whether it can respond to ECFMG electronically rather than by post. The GMC notes most institutions respond within 90 days, but slower responders can take considerably longer.

What documents do I need for EPIC/MyIntealth verification?

Typical documents include your final medical diploma, final medical school transcript, pregraduate internship certificate (where applicable), postgraduate training or specialist qualification certificates if relevant, and your medical registration certificate or licence to practise from your home country's medical regulator. Exact requirements vary by country and by which qualifications the GMC needs verified in your case.

Can I apply for GMC registration before EPIC/MyIntealth verification is complete?

Yes. The GMC allows you to submit your registration application once ECFMG has sent your qualification to your awarding institution for verification, rather than waiting for the full report. However, the GMC can only keep an application open for 90 days, so starting verification well before you apply is strongly recommended.

Does EPIC/MyIntealth verification cost extra on top of GMC fees?

Yes. ECFMG sets and charges its own EPIC fees separately from GMC registration and exam fees, and these are billed in US dollars regardless of which country you are applying from. The exact cost depends on how many qualifications you submit for verification, so check ECFMG's own fee schedule before budgeting.

What happens if my medical school does not respond to the verification request?

If your awarding institution is slow or unresponsive, ECFMG and the GMC generally expect you to follow up directly with your medical school's registrar or alumni office, since ECFMG cannot compel a response. In rare cases where verification cannot be completed within the required timeframe, the GMC will review the reasons and advise on next steps.

Does the verification process differ between countries?

The underlying EPIC/MyIntealth process is identical worldwide, but real-world timelines differ significantly by country and even by individual medical school, depending on how quickly each institution's administrative office responds and whether it can verify electronically. Country-specific guides, such as our walkthroughs for India, Nigeria and Pakistan, cover what candidates from those countries commonly experience.

Do I need a MyIntealth ID for my GMC application?

Yes. Once you establish a MyIntealth account, you are issued a MyIntealth ID, and you must provide this ID when completing your GMC registration application. If you previously used the older EPIC system, you may also have an EPIC ID, which some GMC-facing paperwork still references.

How long is an EPIC/MyIntealth verification report valid for?

Once your qualification is verified, ECFMG can share the report with the GMC, but that permission to share expires if you do not apply for registration within 12 months of the verification being completed. If more than 12 months pass, you will need to ask ECFMG to share the report again before your application can proceed.

What is the single biggest cause of delay in EPIC/MyIntealth verification?

The most common cause of delay is a slow-responding medical school administration office, particularly institutions that only accept verification requests by post or courier rather than electronically. Starting the process as early as possible, ideally months before you plan to submit your GMC application, is the most effective way to avoid this bottleneck.
