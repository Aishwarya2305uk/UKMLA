UKMLA AKT test centres exist across a genuinely global network, which is one of the most reassuring facts for international medical graduates (IMGs) evaluating this pathway for the first time. The Applied Knowledge Test (AKT) is delivered through Pearson VUE, the same commercial test-centre network used for dozens of professional examinations worldwide, which means most candidates can sit the AKT in or near their home country without ever booking a flight. The Clinical and Professional Skills Assessment (CPSA), by contrast, cannot be decentralised in the same way — it is held only at the GMC's purpose-built Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester, with occasional additional London sittings released during periods of especially high demand.

This single contrast — a worldwide network for one exam, a single UK city for the other — is the most important practical fact in this guide, and it shapes almost every planning decision an IMG needs to make. Get it wrong, and you risk assuming both exams can be sat locally, only to discover late in your planning that a mandatory UK trip is unavoidable. This guide sets out exactly where UKMLA AKT test centre locations exist across India, Pakistan, the UK, and other major IMG source regions, how the booking process actually works through GMC Online and Pearson VUE, what to expect on the day itself, and why the CPSA remains centralised while the AKT does not.

Throughout, we cite the GMC's own guidance on the MLA assessments as the authoritative source, and we deliberately avoid presenting any single city list as permanent — Pearson VUE centre availability changes between sitting windows, sometimes significantly, and the only reliable way to confirm a specific location is currently open is through your GMC Online account at the time you are ready to book.

The Quick Answer: Where Can You Sit the UKMLA AKT and CPSA?

The AKT is a computer-based knowledge test delivered through the global Pearson VUE test centre network, so most IMG candidates can sit it in their home country or a nearby regional hub rather than travelling internationally. The CPSA, however, is a face-to-face clinical and communication skills assessment that can only be sat at the GMC's Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester, with occasional supplementary London dates added when demand is unusually high. In practical terms, this means a typical IMG candidate sits the AKT locally — in a city such as Delhi, Lahore, Lagos, or Dubai — and then makes exactly one dedicated trip to the UK later in the pathway purely to complete the CPSA.

This split exists because the two assessments test fundamentally different things. The AKT examines applied medical knowledge and clinical decision-making through several hundred multiple-choice-style questions, a format that Pearson VUE's standardised, secure computer terminals can deliver identically anywhere in the world. The CPSA, however, requires candidates to interact with simulated patients across a circuit of timed stations, assessed in person by trained examiners using a standardised marking framework — something that genuinely cannot be replicated reliably at a rented computer terminal on the other side of the world. Understanding this distinction early prevents the single most common planning error IMG candidates make: assuming that because the AKT is available locally, the CPSA might eventually follow the same model. It will not, at least not under the current assessment design, and budgeting from day one for a UK trip avoids a nasty surprise later in the process.

For a broader look at what the AKT itself actually tests, rather than where it is delivered, see the dedicated guide to AKT format and preparation, and for the full official specification of both assessments, consult the AKT exam pattern pillar page and CPSA exam pattern pillar page directly.

How Booking a UKMLA AKT Test Centre Actually Works

Booking an AKT sitting is not a standalone process — it runs entirely through your GMC Online account, which links directly to the Pearson VUE booking portal once you are confirmed as eligible to sit the exam. Candidates do not book with Pearson VUE independently; instead, GMC Online authorises your eligibility, generates the booking window, and hands you off to Pearson VUE's system to choose a specific date, time, and test centre location.

Step-by-Step: From Eligibility to a Confirmed Seat

  1. Confirm eligibility and create or update your GMC Online account. This account is the single hub for the entire UKMLA pathway, and it is where your booking window opens once your documentation and English language evidence are in order.
  2. Watch for the booking window announcement. The GMC publishes exam sitting periods well in advance, and booking windows for each period open ahead of the sitting itself — often several months prior — so candidates who register their interest early get first access to the widest choice of centres and dates.
  3. Log into GMC Online and follow the link through to the Pearson VUE booking portal. From here you select your preferred country, city, and specific test centre, along with a date and time slot within the current sitting window.
  4. Pay the AKT fee to confirm your booking. Your seat is not secured until payment clears, so avoid a slow bank transfer method close to a popular date if you are worried about losing your preferred slot.
  5. Receive and retain your confirmation, including the exact test centre address and reporting time. Keep this accessible on your phone and in printed form, since you may be asked to show it during check-in.

Because popular centres and dates fill quickly, particularly in high-demand cities such as Delhi/NCR, Lagos, and Dubai, candidates should book as soon as a suitable slot appears rather than waiting for a "better" date closer to their target. In addition, candidates preparing around other commitments — a current job, a visa timeline, or a planned CPSA trip later in the year — should treat the AKT booking window as a hard planning constraint rather than a flexible target, since a missed window typically means waiting for the next sitting period several months later.

Matching Test Centre Bookings to the Wider UKMLA Calendar

Test centre availability does not exist in isolation from the rest of the UKMLA calendar, so it helps to think of booking a specific AKT test centre as one part of a wider scheduling exercise rather than an isolated task. The GMC publishes the exam sitting periods, application deadlines, and results dates for both the AKT and the CPSA well in advance, and cross-referencing these against your own eligibility timeline — EPIC verification, English language test validity, and any visa planning — avoids the common mistake of finding an available test centre but discovering too late that you have missed the application deadline for that particular sitting period. For the full, up-to-date schedule of sitting periods, application windows, and results dates, always check the site's UKMLA key dates pillar page rather than relying on a remembered date from a previous year, since exact dates shift annually.

Furthermore, because booking windows for popular test centres close well before the sitting period itself begins, candidates should treat the published key dates as a countdown to action rather than simply a calendar reference. A candidate who checks the key dates page only once, months before their intended sitting, risks missing the exact week a booking window opens for their preferred city. Revisiting the key dates page periodically as your target sitting period approaches is a simple habit that meaningfully reduces the risk of losing a preferred test centre slot to another candidate.

What to Do if There Is No Test Centre in Your City or Country

Not every candidate lives within easy reach of an open Pearson VUE centre for a given sitting window, and this is a genuinely common situation rather than a rare edge case. If your city or country currently shows no available AKT slot, or if every centre in your usual location is fully booked for your target window, you have several realistic options.

  • Travel to the nearest available centre in a neighbouring city or country. Many candidates from smaller cities or countries with limited Pearson VUE coverage already plan to travel to a regional hub — for example, a candidate from a smaller Gulf state travelling to Dubai, or a candidate from a Pakistani city without a dedicated centre travelling to Lahore or Karachi.
  • Check adjacent sitting windows rather than fixating on one date. Test centre capacity is not evenly distributed across every window; a fully booked date in one period is often more available a few weeks later once the next window opens.
  • Book as early as your eligibility allows. Since seats fill on a first-come basis, candidates who wait until close to a sitting period consistently find the widest and most convenient range of options already gone.
  • Build travel time and cost into your overall budget from the outset, rather than treating it as an unplanned extra expense discovered only once you try to book. This mirrors the same principle used when budgeting for the mandatory Manchester CPSA trip, discussed below.

In addition, candidates in this position should resist the temptation to delay their AKT attempt indefinitely while waiting for a more convenient local centre to appear. Given that centre availability is not guaranteed to improve in any specific timeframe, planning around the nearest currently available centre is usually the more reliable strategy than waiting on the possibility of a new local option opening.

Choosing Between a Local and a Regional Test Centre

Even within a single country, candidates often have a genuine choice between sitting the AKT in their home city or travelling to a larger regional hub, and this decision deserves more thought than it usually gets. A candidate in a smaller Indian city, for example, might weigh a shorter, cheaper journey to a nearby state capital against a longer trip to a larger metropolitan test centre that historically has offered a wider range of dates. Similarly, a candidate based outside the three largest Pakistani cities may need to decide between Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad based on flight availability, cost, and which city has family or friends who can host them overnight before the exam.

Several practical factors are worth weighing when more than one UKMLA AKT test centre option exists within reach. Firstly, consider which centre has historically offered the greater number of available dates within your target sitting window, since a wider spread of dates gives you more flexibility if your preparation timeline shifts. Secondly, factor in the reliability of travel to each option — a short flight with frequent alternatives if one is delayed is often a safer choice than a single long-distance train journey with no realistic backup plan on exam morning. Thirdly, weigh the value of arriving the night before against attempting to travel and sit the exam on the same day; the latter introduces unnecessary risk for very little cost saving, particularly for a high-stakes assessment where a late arrival could mean losing your booking entirely.

For candidates travelling any meaningful distance to a test centre, arriving the previous evening and staying locally overnight is usually the safer choice, even where a same-day journey is theoretically possible. This buffer absorbs minor disruptions — a delayed flight, unexpected traffic, or a late train — without threatening your ability to reach the centre by the required arrival time. Many candidates who have sat the AKT report that the modest additional cost of an overnight stay is a worthwhile trade against the far higher cost, in money and lost time, of missing a booked sitting entirely and having to wait for the next available window.

What the AKT Test Centre Experience Is Like on the Day

The AKT is delivered as a computer-based examination under secure, standardised conditions similar to other major international professional exams run through Pearson VUE. Consequently, the day-to-day experience is broadly consistent regardless of which country or city you sit in, which is itself a reassurance for candidates unfamiliar with the specific venue.

Before You Arrive

Candidates should plan to arrive at the test centre well ahead of their scheduled start time — Pearson VUE centres typically ask candidates to arrive at least 30 minutes before the appointment to allow time for check-in procedures, and arriving late can result in being refused entry and losing the booking entirely. It is worth confirming the exact recommended arrival time on your specific confirmation email or GMC Online booking record, since this can vary slightly by centre.

Identification and Security Procedures

On arrival, candidates must present valid, unexpired photographic identification that matches the name used on their GMC Online booking exactly — typically a passport, since this is the identification document most consistently accepted across Pearson VUE's international network. Centre staff will verify your identity, may take a digital photograph and signature, and will ask you to empty your pockets and store personal belongings, including phones, watches, and study materials, in a secure locker for the duration of the exam. Security procedures at Pearson VUE centres are deliberately strict and standardised worldwide, mirroring the security protocols used for other major professional and academic examinations, in order to protect the integrity of the assessment for every candidate.

During the Exam

Once checked in, candidates are seated at an individual computer terminal in a monitored testing room, with the exam interface displaying questions and answer options on screen and controlled by mouse and keyboard. The room is typically monitored both in person and via CCTV, and candidates are not permitted to communicate with other test-takers or bring in any unauthorised material. Scheduled optional breaks, where the exam format includes them, do not stop the exam clock in most Pearson VUE-delivered assessments, so candidates should plan their pacing with this in mind rather than assuming break time is added on top of the total testing time.

After the Exam

At the end of the sitting, candidates typically hand back any provided materials, retrieve their belongings from the secure locker, and leave the centre. Results are not usually available immediately at the centre itself; instead, they are communicated through GMC Online according to the GMC's published results timeline for that sitting period.

Common Exam-Day Logistics Questions

Beyond the formal check-in and security steps, several practical logistics questions come up repeatedly among first-time AKT candidates. Parking and public transport access vary considerably between individual Pearson VUE locations, so it is worth checking the specific centre's address and transport options in the days before your sitting rather than assuming every centre is easily reached by the same method. Similarly, food and drink are not usually permitted inside the testing room itself, so candidates should eat beforehand and plan around any scheduled breaks rather than expecting to bring refreshments to the desk. Finally, candidates with a disability or a condition requiring reasonable adjustments — extra time, a separate room, or assistive equipment — should raise this with the GMC well in advance of booking, since adjustments are arranged through the GMC rather than negotiated directly with the test centre on the day.

Rescheduling and Cancellation: What to Expect

Circumstances change, and candidates sometimes need to reschedule or cancel a booked AKT sitting. Rather than quoting a specific deadline or fee here — since these are exactly the kind of detail that shifts between sitting periods and that this guide cannot promise remains accurate by the time you read it — the safest approach is to treat GMC Online and the Pearson VUE booking portal as the only authoritative source for current rescheduling and cancellation terms. In general terms, professional testing providers such as Pearson VUE typically apply a tiered system where changes made well in advance of the exam date carry little or no penalty, while changes made close to the sitting date carry a higher fee or may not be possible at all. Candidates who anticipate any uncertainty around their availability — for example, a pending visa decision, a work rota that has not yet been confirmed, or ongoing travel restrictions — should check the exact current policy before booking, rather than assuming standard consumer-friendly terms will apply. This is a case where verifying the live policy directly protects both your money and your exam date.

Why the CPSA Cannot Be Decentralised Like the AKT

It is worth explaining explicitly why the CPSA remains fixed to Manchester rather than following the AKT's global model, since candidates unfamiliar with the format sometimes assume this is simply an administrative choice that could change at any time. In practice, it reflects a genuine structural difference between the two assessments.

The AKT is a knowledge test: it presents standardised questions on a secure computer terminal, and delivering it correctly requires nothing more than a controlled room, a monitored terminal, and a trained invigilator — infrastructure that Pearson VUE already operates identically in hundreds of cities worldwide for many different examinations. The CPSA, however, is a simulated clinical encounter: candidates rotate through a circuit of timed stations, each involving a trained role-player acting as a patient, and a qualified examiner assessing the candidate's clinical reasoning, communication, and professionalism in real time against a standardised marking framework. Reproducing this reliably requires purpose-built clinical simulation space, a large pool of trained role-players and examiners working to identical standards, and rigorous quality assurance across every station and every sitting — none of which can be outsourced to a generic computer-testing vendor the way a knowledge exam can.

Consequently, the GMC has chosen to concentrate this infrastructure at a single, purpose-built Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester rather than attempting to replicate it at dozens of international sites, which would risk inconsistent marking standards between locations. Occasional additional CPSA sittings in London are added during particularly high-demand periods, but this remains an extension of the same centralised model rather than a step toward a genuinely global network like the AKT's. For IMG candidates, the practical implication is straightforward: budget for exactly one mandatory UK trip — flights, a UK visa if required, several nights of accommodation, and living costs during preparation and the assessment itself — as a fixed, non-negotiable part of the pathway, rather than something that might be avoided with enough searching for a local alternative.

Budgeting for the Manchester Trip Once Your AKT Test Centre Sitting Is Passed

Because the AKT can be sat locally while the CPSA cannot, candidates sometimes budget carefully for the exam fees themselves but underestimate the surrounding travel costs of the Manchester stage. A realistic budget for the UK trip should include return flights from your home country, a UK visitor visa application if your nationality requires one, several nights of accommodation in or near Manchester for preparation and the assessment itself, local transport, and food and incidental costs for the duration of the stay. Many candidates also choose to arrive several days early to adjust to the time zone, attend a short preparation course, or simply settle their nerves before the assessment, all of which add further nights of accommodation to the total. Treating this trip as a single, clearly budgeted line item — planned as far in advance as your AKT test centre booking — rather than an afterthought once the CPSA becomes bookable, avoids the scramble for funds that many first-time candidates report experiencing.

Once both assessments are passed, the remaining steps to full GMC registration are administrative rather than exam-related, and the site's dedicated GMC registration guide walks through exactly what happens next — document submission, processing times, and what to do if your application is queried. Reading this guide alongside your CPSA travel planning helps you sequence the final stages of the pathway correctly, since some registration documentation can be prepared and submitted while you are still finalising your Manchester travel arrangements.

Planning Around Limited Sitting Windows

Because both the AKT and the CPSA are only offered during defined sitting periods rather than on demand, planning around these windows is as important as preparing for the exam content itself. Candidates should treat the overall UKMLA timeline as bounded by sitting-window availability, not simply by how quickly they finish revising. In addition, since the CPSA can only be booked once the AKT has been passed, a missed or delayed AKT sitting has a knock-on effect on the entire remaining timeline, potentially pushing a candidate's CPSA attempt back by several months rather than a few weeks. For this reason, experienced advisers consistently recommend booking the earliest realistic AKT sitting available to you, rather than waiting for a date that feels more comfortable relative to your revision schedule, since the downstream cost of delay is usually higher than the benefit of a few extra weeks of preparation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take the UKMLA AKT in India?

Yes. Pearson VUE has historically maintained AKT-suitable test centres in several major Indian cities, including Delhi/NCR, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, and Kolkata. However, exact centre availability changes between sitting windows, so Indian candidates should always confirm the live, current list through their GMC Online account or the Pearson VUE booking portal before finalising travel plans.

Are there UKMLA AKT test centres in Pakistan?

Yes. Pakistani candidates have typically had access to Pearson VUE centres in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad, covering the country's three largest cities. As with every country covered in this guide, candidates should verify current availability directly through GMC Online rather than relying on a previous sitting's centre list, since availability is not guaranteed to remain identical between testing periods.

Do I have to travel to the UK for the CPSA?

Yes, for every candidate worldwide. The CPSA is held exclusively at the GMC's Clinical Assessment Centre in Manchester, with occasional additional London dates released during high-demand periods, and there is no remote, computer-based, or overseas alternative. Every IMG candidate should budget for at least one dedicated UK trip to complete this stage of the pathway, regardless of where they sat the AKT.

How do I book a UKMLA AKT test centre?

Booking runs entirely through your GMC Online account, which links to the Pearson VUE booking portal once your eligibility is confirmed. You select your preferred country, city, specific test centre, and time slot within the current sitting window, then pay the exam fee to confirm your seat. Because popular centres and dates fill quickly, it is best to book as soon as a suitable slot becomes available rather than waiting closer to the sitting period.

What happens if there is no AKT test centre near me?

If your city or country has no currently available centre, or every local slot is fully booked, the realistic options are travelling to the nearest available centre in a neighbouring city or country, or checking a later sitting window where capacity may have opened up. Candidates in this position should book as early as their eligibility allows, since demand for the nearest alternative centre is often high among other candidates facing the same gap.

What should I bring to the AKT test centre on exam day?

You should bring valid, unexpired photographic identification, typically a passport, matching the name on your GMC Online booking exactly, along with your exam confirmation details. Personal items including phones, watches, and study materials are not permitted in the testing room and must be stored in a secure locker provided by the centre, and candidates should plan to arrive at least 30 minutes before their scheduled appointment time.

Can UKMLA AKT test centre availability change between sitting windows?

Yes, and this is one of the most important practical points in this entire guide. Pearson VUE opens, closes, and reallocates specific test centres in response to demand and operational factors, so a city that hosted a centre during one sitting period may have reduced capacity, a different specific venue, or no centre at all during a later period. Always confirm the live, current list through GMC Online or the Pearson VUE booking portal at the time you are ready to book, rather than relying on any previously published list, including the one in this guide.

Is the CPSA ever offered outside Manchester?

Occasionally. The GMC has, during periods of especially high demand, released additional CPSA sitting dates in London alongside the standard Manchester schedule. However, this remains an extension of the same centralised model rather than a step toward decentralising the assessment the way the AKT already is, so candidates should still expect Manchester to be the default and most consistently available location.

Finding the right UKMLA AKT test centre is usually far simpler than candidates initially expect, thanks to Pearson VUE's genuinely global network across India, Pakistan, the UK, and most other major IMG source regions. Always confirm the current, live test centre list through GMC Online before booking either assessment, since availability shifts between sitting windows in ways no static guide can fully anticipate.
