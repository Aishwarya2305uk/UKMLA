The UKMLA exam booking process is the practical, mechanical step that comes after eligibility is confirmed — and it catches out more candidates than almost any other part of the pathway, simply because it is treated as an afterthought rather than a task with its own deadlines, quirks, and failure modes. Whether you are a UK final-year medical student whose Applied Knowledge Test (AKT) sitting is coordinated by your medical school, or an international medical graduate (IMG) booking the AKT through Pearson VUE and the Clinical and Professional Skills Assessment (CPSA) directly with the GMC, the booking stage is where good planning either pays off or falls apart. This guide walks through exactly how to book each component, in what order, through which system, and what to do when a slot fills up, a date needs to change, or you need adjustments in place before you book at all.

This is a narrower, more transactional guide than a general registration or eligibility overview — it assumes you already know you are eligible to sit the AKT and/or CPSA and simply need to understand the mechanics of turning that eligibility into a confirmed seat on a confirmed date. If you have not yet confirmed your eligibility, start with the full GMC registration guide first, then come back here once you are ready to book. Understanding the UKMLA exam booking process before you need it, rather than while you're under time pressure, is what separates candidates who secure their preferred date from those left scrambling for whatever slot remains.

The Quick Answer: How Does UKMLA Exam Booking Actually Work?

The UKMLA exam booking process runs almost entirely through GMC Online, the GMC's account portal, though the exact mechanics differ sharply depending on which exam and which audience you belong to. UK final-year students typically have their AKT sitting coordinated centrally by their medical school within a single national testing window, so most of the individual booking mechanics are handled on their behalf. IMGs, by contrast, book the AKT individually through GMC Online, which hands off to the Pearson VUE scheduling system for a specific test centre, date, and time. The CPSA is different again: it is organised directly by the GMC at its own dedicated assessment centres — most commonly in Manchester — with a separate booking and scheduling process from the AKT, opened only once a candidate has the right AKT result or eligibility status in place.

Across every route, three things stay constant: eligibility must be confirmed before a booking screen ever becomes visible, popular dates and test centres fill quickly once a booking window opens, and rescheduling or cancelling a confirmed booking almost always carries either a fee, a notice-period requirement, or both. Keeping these three constants in mind is worth more than memorising any specific date, because the specific dates change every year while the underlying booking logic does not.

For the definitive, always-current list of sitting periods and booking-window opening dates, treat the site's own UKMLA key dates pillar page as your primary planning reference rather than any single blog post, since exact dates shift from year to year.

It helps to think of the wider UKMLA exam booking process as three separate but related booking journeys rather than a single uniform system: the UK-student AKT journey, the IMG AKT journey, and the IMG CPSA journey. Each has its own portal behaviour, its own typical lead time, and its own failure points, and conflating them is one of the most common sources of confusion candidates report. The sections below treat each journey in turn, in the order most candidates actually encounter them.

Booking the AKT as a UK Medical Student

UK medical students preparing to sit the AKT experience a fundamentally different UKMLA exam booking process from IMGs, because the mechanics are largely handled at institutional level rather than by the individual candidate logging into a public booking portal.

Step 1: Your Medical School Registers You for the National Sitting

UK medical schools coordinate their final-year (and in some programmes, penultimate-year) students into a single national AKT sitting window, agreed centrally with the GMC and delivered through Pearson VUE's UK test centre network. In practice, this means your medical school's exams office — not you personally — submits your candidate details ahead of the sitting, confirms your eligibility to sit alongside your cohort, and allocates you to a test centre and time slot within the national window. You will typically receive this information through your university's student portal or exams office communications rather than by logging into a public consumer-style booking page yourself.

Step 2: Confirm Your Personal Details Are Correct

Even though your medical school manages the bulk of the process, you retain responsibility for making sure the personal details your school holds — full legal name, date of birth, any additional support requirements — exactly match your identity documents and any GMC Online profile you may already hold. A mismatch between your university records and your official identity documents is one of the most common, entirely avoidable causes of last-minute confusion on exam day, so check this proactively rather than assuming the data on file is correct.

Step 3: Watch for Your School's Internal Deadlines

Because UK students are coordinated in bulk, the deadlines that matter to you are usually internal ones set by your medical school — the date by which you must confirm attendance, declare any extenuating circumstances, or submit a reasonable-adjustments request — rather than a public GMC booking-window date. These internal deadlines are frequently earlier than the equivalent public deadlines IMGs face, precisely because your school needs time to aggregate and submit the whole cohort's information to the GMC in one batch. Treat any deadline your exams office sets as firm; a late request is far harder to accommodate once your school has already submitted its cohort list.

Step 4: Confirm Your Test Centre and Reporting Time

Once your school has processed the national allocation, you will receive confirmation of your specific test centre (often, though not always, on or near your own university campus), your reporting time, and any instructions about identification and permitted items. Read this confirmation carefully — reporting times can vary between students at the same institution depending on which session or circuit they have been allocated to, so do not assume you share an identical schedule with every classmate.

Step 5: Know Who to Contact If Something Changes

If your circumstances change close to the sitting — illness, a family emergency, or a newly diagnosed condition requiring adjustments — your first point of contact should always be your medical school's exams office, not the GMC directly. Your school acts as the intermediary for the national sitting and is best placed to escalate an individual change quickly, since it already holds the relevant cohort-level booking relationship with the GMC and Pearson VUE.

UK students who want a fuller picture of how their institution's own implementation of the assessment works — since delivery details vary meaningfully between universities — should read the guide on how individual UK medical schools implement the UKMLA alongside this one.

Booking the AKT as an IMG

International medical graduates face a very different UKMLA exam booking process: rather than a school handling matters on your behalf, you are personally responsible for creating your own booking, choosing your own test centre, and paying your own exam fee, all through your individual GMC Online account.

Step 1: Confirm Eligibility and Complete Your GMC Online Profile

Before any booking screen becomes available to you, your GMC Online account must show you as eligible to sit the AKT — meaning your primary medical qualification has been verified, your English language evidence is valid and current, and any outstanding identity checks are complete. Attempting to jump ahead to booking before this status is confirmed simply will not work; the booking option does not appear until the GMC's own systems mark you as cleared to proceed. If you have not yet reached this stage, the step-by-step registration walkthrough covers the verification and eligibility process in full detail.

Step 2: Watch for the Booking Window to Open

The GMC publishes AKT sitting periods well in advance, and for each sitting period there is a distinct booking window — a defined stretch of time during which eligible candidates can actually select and pay for a slot. These windows open ahead of the sitting itself, often by a period of months, and candidates who register their interest and check their GMC Online account regularly around the expected opening date get first access to the widest range of test centres and appointment times. Missing the opening of a booking window does not necessarily mean missing the sitting entirely, but it does mean starting your search for a slot only after the best-located and most convenient options have already been taken by earlier candidates.

Step 3: Move from GMC Online into the Pearson VUE Scheduling System

Once your booking window is open, GMC Online hands you off to Pearson VUE's scheduling system, where the actual date, time, and test-centre selection takes place. This is a genuinely important distinction to understand: GMC Online is where your eligibility lives and where the UKMLA exam booking process begins, but Pearson VUE is the system that physically manages test-centre capacity, calendar availability, and appointment confirmation. You do not create a separate Pearson VUE account from scratch in isolation — the link from GMC Online carries your verified eligibility across — but you should expect the interface itself to look and feel like a standard Pearson VUE test-scheduling page rather than the GMC's own site design.

Step 4: Select a Test Centre and Appointment Slot

Within Pearson VUE's scheduler, you will typically be able to compare a small number of test centres near your preferred location alongside their available dates and times within the current sitting window. Because the AKT is delivered through Pearson VUE's global commercial test-centre network, candidates in high-volume countries — India, Pakistan, Nigeria, and Gulf states among them — usually have a genuine choice of city, while candidates in lower-volume regions may find only one or two realistic options. Pearson VUE publishes its own general guidance for test-takers on its GMC test-booking portal, which is worth bookmarking as the authoritative reference for how its scheduling interface itself behaves, separate from the GMC's own eligibility rules. For a country-by-country breakdown of where AKT test centres have typically existed, see the dedicated guide to AKT test centre locations, though centre availability does shift between sitting windows, so always confirm the live list inside your own booking session rather than relying on any previously published overview.

Step 5: Pay the Exam Fee to Confirm Your Booking

Your seat is not secured until payment is made and clears — an unconfirmed selection can be released back into the general pool if payment is not completed promptly, so avoid slow payment methods when booking a popular date. Fees are reviewed periodically by the GMC, so always confirm the current AKT fee on your GMC Online account or the GMC's own published fee schedule before booking, rather than relying on a figure quoted in an earlier guide or forum post, since exact charges do change from one year to the next.

Step 6: Save Your Confirmation and Admission Details

Once payment clears, you will receive a booking confirmation with your test centre address, reporting time, and any identification requirements for the day. Save this in at least two places — a printed copy and a digital backup — and check it again close to your sitting date in case any administrative detail has changed since booking.

Technical and Practical Requirements for Booking Online

Every stage of the UKMLA exam booking process — whether you are a UK student confirming details through your university portal or an IMG completing a booking through GMC Online and Pearson VUE — happens on standard browser-based systems, and a small amount of technical preparation avoids an entirely unnecessary last-minute panic.

Use a Reliable Device, Browser, and Connection

Book from a desktop or laptop computer with an up-to-date browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari) and a stable internet connection, rather than attempting the process on a mobile device or an unreliable public network. Booking interfaces for both GMC Online and Pearson VUE are designed and tested primarily for desktop use, and mobile browsers occasionally render selection screens or payment forms incorrectly, which can cost you a popular slot during the critical first minutes after a booking window opens.

Have Your Payment Method Ready in Advance

Whichever exam you are booking, have a working debit or credit card ready before you begin selecting a date, rather than searching for a card or authorising international payments only once you have already chosen a slot. Payment authorisation delays are one of the most common, entirely preventable reasons a candidate loses a slot to another candidate during a high-demand booking window.

Save Confirmation Immediately, Not Later

Once a booking is confirmed — through whichever route applies to you — save or screenshot the confirmation page immediately, before navigating away or closing the browser tab. If the confirmation page fails to load fully, check your registered email address for a confirmation message before attempting to book again, since re-attempting a booking that has, in fact, already succeeded can create a duplicate-booking complication that takes time and correspondence with the GMC to unwind.

Understanding the Fees Involved in Booking

Cost is a real, practical part of the UKMLA exam booking process, and candidates budgeting for the AKT and CPSA should treat published figures as a snapshot rather than a permanent number, since the GMC reviews its fee schedule periodically. As a general rule of thumb reflecting recent fee levels, the AKT fee for IMG candidates has sat in the region of the mid-to-high £200s per sitting, while the CPSA carries a substantially higher fee reflecting the cost of running a face-to-face, examiner-staffed clinical assessment at a dedicated centre. UK students should check with their medical school whether any element of the AKT fee is charged directly to them or absorbed into wider course costs, since this varies between institutions.

Whatever the exact current figures are at the point you book, always verify them on your own GMC Online account or the GMC's official fees page immediately before paying, rather than relying on a number quoted in this or any other guide — fee schedules are reviewed periodically and figures can and do change between sittings. For a full breakdown of every fee across the entire pathway, not just the booking stage, see the site's dedicated GMC registration guide, which covers registration, verification, and exam costs together.

Common Mistakes That Delay or Derail a Booking

Certain avoidable errors recur again and again across both the UK-student and IMG routes through the UKMLA exam booking process, and learning to recognise them in advance is the cheapest possible form of insurance against losing time or money.

  • Waiting to "feel ready" before booking. Especially for the CPSA, where capacity is genuinely tight, candidates who wait until they feel fully prepared before booking frequently find no suitable date left in their preferred window. Book early and prepare towards the date, not the other way around.
  • Mismatched personal details across systems. A name, date of birth, or nationality that differs even slightly between your medical school's records, your GMC Online profile, and your passport can flag your booking for manual review and cause delay. Reconcile these details before you start any booking process, not after a problem arises.
  • Assuming a "reschedule" option exists. As covered above, most systems require a full cancellation and rebooking rather than a simple date change, and candidates who assume otherwise are sometimes caught out by an unexpected fee.
  • Leaving reasonable-adjustments requests until after booking. Adjustment applications need lead time of their own, separate from the exam booking window itself, and should never be submitted as an afterthought once a slot is already secured.
  • Underestimating visa lead time for the CPSA. IMG candidates who book a CPSA date without first checking realistic visa processing timelines for their nationality risk a booking they cannot actually attend.
  • Trusting unofficial "booking assistance" services. Any agent or paid service claiming to secure, expedite, or transfer a GMC exam booking on your behalf is not operating through an official channel — all genuine bookings must be completed by the candidate directly through their own GMC Online account.

Booking the CPSA as an IMG

The CPSA follows an entirely separate booking track from the AKT within the overall UKMLA exam booking process, run directly by the GMC rather than through the Pearson VUE network, because it is a face-to-face clinical and communication skills assessment rather than a computer-delivered knowledge test.

Step 1: Become Eligible to Book the CPSA

For most IMG candidates, eligibility to book the CPSA follows directly from achieving the required outcome in the AKT (or, under the legacy PLAB route, passing PLAB 1). Only once this status is reflected in your GMC Online account does the CPSA booking option become available to you — there is no route to booking the CPSA ahead of, or independently from, satisfying this prerequisite.

Step 2: Book Directly Through GMC Online — Not Pearson VUE

Unlike the AKT, the CPSA booking process does not route you to a third-party scheduling system. Instead, you select and confirm your CPSA sitting directly within GMC Online, because the GMC organises and delivers the CPSA itself at its own dedicated assessment centres rather than through a commercial test-centre network. This is a meaningful practical difference from the AKT booking flow, and candidates who assume the two processes will feel identical are sometimes caught off guard by how much more centralised the CPSA booking experience is.

Step 3: Understand Why the CPSA Is Geographically Centralised

The CPSA is held at the GMC's purpose-built Clinical Assessment Centre, most commonly cited as being located in Manchester, with occasional additional sittings released elsewhere during periods of unusually high demand. This centralisation exists because the assessment requires candidates to interact with simulated patients across a circuit of timed clinical stations, marked in person by trained examiners using a standardised framework — something that cannot be delivered reliably at a rented computer terminal in the way the AKT can. In practical terms, this means the large majority of IMG candidates need to plan and budget for a dedicated trip to the UK specifically to sit the CPSA, even if they were able to sit the AKT entirely from their home country.

Step 4: Book Early — CPSA Capacity Is Tighter Than the AKT

Because the CPSA can only be delivered at a small number of physical locations with a genuinely finite number of assessor-staffed circuits running on any given day, capacity fills considerably faster than AKT capacity in the wider Pearson VUE network. As soon as your GMC Online account confirms you are eligible to book, treat that as the moment to act — even candidates who are not yet fully prepared to sit the assessment often benefit from securing a date early and building their revision timeline backwards from it, rather than waiting until they feel "ready" and finding no suitable date left in a convenient window.

Step 5: Plan Your UK Travel Around the Confirmed Date

Once your CPSA date is confirmed, begin your visa, travel, and accommodation planning immediately rather than waiting until closer to the sitting. Visa processing times vary considerably by country and by the specific visa route you are travelling under, and a CPSA booking with insufficient lead time for visa processing creates a genuinely difficult situation with very few good options. Build in a buffer beyond the minimum estimated visa processing time wherever possible.

Rescheduling and Cancellation: What to Expect

Circumstances change, and understanding the practical consequences of rescheduling or cancelling a UKMLA booking before you need to do it will save both money and stress if the situation ever arises.

Cancellation Fees Are Usually Tiered by Notice Period

Rather than a single flat cancellation fee, GMC and Pearson VUE cancellation policies for AKT and CPSA bookings typically apply a tiered refund structure based on how much notice you give before your original sitting date. Candidates who cancel well in advance of their sitting generally receive a significantly larger proportion of their fee back than those who cancel close to the exam date, and cancelling with only a short amount of notice before the sitting commonly results in little or no refund at all. The exact thresholds and percentages are reviewed periodically, so always check the current cancellation policy published on your GMC Online account or the GMC's own website at the time you book, rather than assuming a figure quoted in an older guide still applies.

There Is Usually No Direct "Reschedule" Button

A detail that surprises many candidates: most AKT and CPSA booking systems do not offer a simple one-click reschedule function that moves an existing booking to a new date while keeping the original payment in place. Instead, changing your date typically means cancelling your existing booking (incurring whatever cancellation fee applies based on your notice period) and then booking again at the currently published fee for a new available slot. This two-step mechanic is worth understanding clearly before you ever need to use it, since candidates sometimes assume a "reschedule" request will be cheaper or simpler than it actually is in practice.

Seats Cannot Usually Be Transferred Between Candidates

If you cancel a confirmed booking, your seat does not transfer to another candidate — it returns to the general pool of available slots, where anyone monitoring that sitting window can book it. There is no mechanism for privately transferring or "gifting" a booked slot to a specific other candidate, and any suggestion otherwise from an unofficial source should be treated with suspicion.

Booking Early Reduces Your Exposure to All of the Above

The single most effective way to avoid an expensive or stressful rescheduling situation is to book conservatively in the first place — choosing a date with a comfortable margin around known commitments, visa timelines, and other exams, rather than the earliest theoretically possible date. A slightly later, better-buffered booking is almost always preferable to an aggressive early booking that later needs to be cancelled and rebooked at a fee.

Requesting Reasonable Adjustments Before You Book

If you require reasonable adjustments — extra time, a separate room, assistive technology, or any other accommodation — the timing of your request matters as much as the request itself.

Apply for Adjustments Before, Not After, Booking a Slot

Reasonable-adjustment applications generally need to be submitted, assessed, and approved ahead of the point at which you book a specific test date, because approved adjustments can affect which test centres or session types are suitable for you, and because processing an adjustment request takes real administrative time that a last-minute booking simply does not allow for. Submitting a reasonable-adjustments request only after you have already secured a slot risks a mismatch between your confirmed booking and the accommodation you actually need, which can be far harder to resolve retroactively than to arrange correctly from the outset.

Build In Extra Lead Time Beyond the Standard Booking Window

Because adjustment requests require supporting evidence to be reviewed on an individual, case-by-case basis, candidates who need adjustments should plan to submit their request and supporting documentation considerably earlier than the point at which they would otherwise start looking at booking windows. Leaving an adjustment request until the same week you intend to book a slot is one of the most common avoidable planning mistakes candidates with genuine support needs make.

The Same Principle Applies to Both the AKT and the CPSA

Adjustments are assessed separately for each component of the assessment, so a candidate sitting both the AKT and the CPSA should not assume that an adjustment approved for one automatically carries across to the other. Plan and submit evidence for each component's adjustment request on its own timeline, well ahead of booking either sitting.

How the AKT and CPSA Booking Processes Compare

Although both are components of the same overall assessment, the AKT and CPSA booking processes differ in almost every practical respect once you look past the shared starting point of a GMC Online account. The AKT, delivered through Pearson VUE's global network, generally offers more test-centre choice and shorter lead times to secure a seat, precisely because there are simply more available slots spread across more physical locations worldwide. The CPSA, delivered only by the GMC itself at a small number of dedicated centres, offers far less flexibility of both location and date, and rewards candidates who act the moment they become eligible rather than those who wait for an ideal-seeming date.

This distinction also affects how you should sequence your preparation. Because CPSA capacity is the tighter constraint of the two, many successful IMG candidates book their CPSA date as soon as they are eligible — even before they feel fully prepared — and then work their clinical and communication skills revision backwards from that fixed date, rather than preparing first and searching for a booking slot afterwards. For background on how the two assessments differ in content and purpose, rather than in booking mechanics, see the site's guides to the AKT exam pattern and the CPSA exam pattern.

It is also worth understanding how this two-exam structure fits into the GMC's broader modernisation of IMG licensing, since candidates familiar with the legacy PLAB system sometimes carry assumptions from that route into UKMLA booking that no longer apply cleanly. The guide on how the UKMLA relates to the legacy PLAB pathway explains exactly what has changed and what has stayed the same across the transition.

What Happens After You Book

Once your booking is confirmed — whether that is an AKT slot allocated through your medical school, an AKT slot self-booked through Pearson VUE, or a CPSA date booked directly with the GMC — your focus should shift immediately to two parallel tracks: practical logistics and substantive preparation.

On the logistics side, IMG candidates in particular should begin any necessary visa application immediately after a CPSA booking is confirmed, since visa processing timelines can run into the months depending on nationality and route, and a confirmed exam date with an unresolved visa is a genuinely stressful position to be in as the sitting approaches. UK students should confirm accommodation and travel arrangements if their allocated test centre is not on their home campus.

On the preparation side, candidates who have just booked — rather than candidates still waiting for a booking window — are in the strongest position to build a realistic, date-anchored study plan, since a confirmed sitting date turns preparation from an open-ended task into one with a fixed endpoint. New GMC registrants and newly arrived IMGs starting their UK practice should also review the GMC's welcome-to-UK-practice induction guidance alongside their exam preparation, since induction and assessment planning increasingly overlap for candidates arriving close to their CPSA sitting.

Candidates who work through the UKMLA exam booking process methodically — confirming eligibility, watching for the right window, booking with a sensible buffer, and treating adjustments and visas as separate lead-time items — consistently report a smoother run-up to their sitting than candidates who treat booking as a five-minute task to be squeezed in between other commitments. The administrative discipline required at this stage is, in a small way, good practice for the kind of careful, deadline-aware working habits the rest of UK medical practice will expect of you.

Frequently Asked Questions About the UKMLA Exam Booking Process

Do UK medical students book their own AKT sitting?

No — UK final-year (and in some programmes, penultimate-year) medical students do not typically book an individual AKT slot themselves through a public booking portal. Instead, their medical school coordinates the entire cohort into a single national sitting window, submitting candidate details to the GMC and allocating students to specific test centres and times. Students are still responsible for confirming their personal details are accurate and meeting any internal deadlines their school sets for attendance confirmation or adjustment requests.

Can I book the AKT directly with Pearson VUE without going through GMC Online first?

No — IMG candidates cannot create an independent Pearson VUE booking for the AKT without first being confirmed as eligible through GMC Online. GMC Online verifies your eligibility and opens your booking window, and only then hands you off to the Pearson VUE scheduling system to select a specific test centre, date, and time. Attempting to access Pearson VUE's scheduler without this prior authorisation will not produce a valid AKT booking option.

Why is the CPSA only available in Manchester?

The CPSA is a face-to-face clinical and communication skills assessment requiring candidates to interact with simulated patients across a circuit of timed stations, marked in person by trained examiners using a standardised framework. This kind of assessment cannot be delivered reliably through a commercial computer-based test-centre network in the way the AKT can, so the GMC organises and delivers it itself at its own purpose-built Clinical Assessment Centre, most commonly cited as being in Manchester, with occasional additional sittings released elsewhere during periods of high demand.

What happens if I need to cancel my AKT or CPSA booking?

Cancellation policies for both exams typically apply a tiered refund structure based on how much notice you give before your sitting date — cancelling well in advance generally returns a larger proportion of your fee than cancelling close to the exam date, and very late cancellations often receive little or no refund. There is usually no direct "reschedule" function; changing your date generally means cancelling your existing booking, incurring whatever fee applies, and booking a new slot separately at the currently published fee.

How early should I request reasonable adjustments before booking?

Reasonable-adjustment applications should be submitted, assessed, and approved before you attempt to book a specific test date, since approved adjustments can affect which test centres or session types suit you, and because processing this kind of application takes genuine administrative time. Build in considerably more lead time than you think you need, and never wait until the same week you plan to book to submit supporting evidence.

Can someone else book my UKMLA exam sitting for me?

For UK students, your medical school's exams office effectively manages the UKMLA exam booking process on your behalf as part of the national AKT sitting, but you remain responsible for ensuring your personal details are accurate. For IMGs booking either the AKT or the CPSA, the booking must be completed under your own verified GMC Online account — there is no official mechanism for a third party, agent, or paid service to complete a valid booking for you, and any service claiming to do so should be treated as operating outside official channels.

What should I do if my preferred AKT or CPSA date is fully booked?

If your preferred date or test centre shows no availability, check alternative nearby test centres (for the AKT) or the next available date within the current sitting window, since cancellations by other candidates occasionally release seats back into the pool without advance notice. If no suitable slot exists anywhere within the current window, you will typically need to wait for the next published sitting period — which is exactly why booking as early as possible once your window opens matters so much.

Conclusion

The mechanics of the UKMLA exam booking process are straightforward once you understand which system applies to you — medical-school coordination for UK students' AKT sittings, GMC Online through to Pearson VUE for IMG AKT bookings, and a direct GMC-run process for the CPSA — but the margin for costly mistakes is real if you leave booking, adjustment requests, or rescheduling decisions until the last minute. Confirm your eligibility first, watch for booking windows to open, book conservatively with a sensible buffer around other commitments, and submit any reasonable-adjustments request well ahead of booking a slot. Getting the booking stage right frees you to focus entirely on preparation once your date is confirmed.
