Understanding how to register for PLAB 1 is the first practical hurdle every international medical graduate faces on the road to GMC registration in the UK. The process involves multiple stages across different platforms — from credential verification through MyIntealth to booking your sitting via GMC Online — and each stage has its own timeline and potential for delay. This comprehensive guide walks you through every step in sequence, with practical tips for avoiding the most common mistakes that cause IMGs to lose months to administrative errors.

PLAB 1 is a three-hour written examination comprising 180 single-best-answer (SBA) questions. It assesses clinical knowledge across the breadth of UK medical practice and forms Part 1 of the licensing assessment for IMGs. Passing PLAB 1 allows you to proceed to PLAB 2 (the OSCE), and passing both allows you to apply for GMC provisional registration. As of 2025, PLAB is transitioning to become the IMG pathway of the UKMLA — the same Applied Knowledge Test (AKT) that UK medical students sit — so the content and process will align further over the coming years.

Step 1: Confirm Your PLAB 1 Eligibility

Before you begin any application process, confirm that you meet the eligibility criteria to register for PLAB 1. The core requirements are: holding an acceptable primary medical qualification (PMQ), having English language proficiency at the GMC standard, and not having exhausted your PLAB 1 attempt limit.

Your PMQ must come from a medical school listed on the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS) and assessed as acceptable by the GMC. This covers the majority of recognised medical schools worldwide, but not all — check wdoms.org to confirm your school is listed. If your school is listed, that is a necessary but not always sufficient condition; the GMC assesses qualifications from certain countries with additional scrutiny.

If you have already attempted PLAB 1 four times, you are at the standard attempt limit. A fifth attempt is possible if you have completed additional clinical training approved by the GMC. If you are in this position, you must contact the GMC directly before attempting to book a further sitting.

Review the full eligibility requirements for a detailed breakdown of accepted qualifications and English language requirements before proceeding.

Step 2: Complete MyIntealth Verification

The credential verification step is the longest and most unpredictable part of the registration process. Until 2024, this was handled through EPIC (Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials). EPIC has now been replaced by MyIntealth, the new platform developed by Intealth (the parent organisation of ECFMG). All new applicants must use MyIntealth.

How Long Does MyIntealth Verification Take?

Verification timelines vary significantly. For candidates whose medical schools respond quickly, verification can be completed in four to six weeks. For candidates whose schools are slow, have merged, have changed names, or have incomplete records, the process can take three to six months or longer. Build the worst-case scenario into your planning — assume six months and treat anything faster as a bonus.

Step-by-Step MyIntealth Process

  1. Visit the MyIntealth website and create an account using your email address and a government-issued ID.
  2. Enter your personal details exactly as they appear on your medical degree certificate — name, date of birth, and nationality.
  3. Enter your medical school's details and graduation year. If your school has merged or changed its name, use the name under which you graduated.
  4. Upload certified copies of your degree certificate, your academic transcript, and any postgraduate qualifications you want verified.
  5. Select the General Medical Council (GMC) as a receiving organisation so results are sent directly to the GMC on your behalf.
  6. Pay the verification fee. Fees depend on the number and type of credentials being verified.
  7. Monitor the "My Cases" section of your MyIntealth dashboard. You will receive email notifications as each credential is verified.
  8. Once verification is complete, the GMC receives the results automatically. No action is required from you at this stage beyond confirming receipt in your GMC Online account.

Common Verification Issues by Country

  • India: Name discrepancies between degree and passport are common. Ensure all documents use the exact same name spelling.
  • Nigeria: Delays often occur when MDCN (Medical and Dental Council of Nigeria) records need to be cross-referenced. Begin early.
  • Pakistan: PMDC records can have delays, particularly for older graduates. Allow extra time.
  • Egypt: Arabic-language documents may require certified translation. Budget additional cost and time for translation.
  • Caribbean schools: Some schools have limited administrative capacity for verification requests. Follow up directly with your school if verification stalls.

Step 3: Meet English Language Requirements

The GMC requires proof of English language proficiency through either IELTS Academic or OET Medicine. Both tests must have been completed within two years of your GMC application date.

For IELTS Academic, you need an overall score of 7.5 with no individual skill below 7.0. For OET Medicine, you need Grade B (350 or above) in all four sub-tests. The tests assess Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking — all four must meet the minimum in a single sitting (though the GMC allows combining two sittings within six months in limited circumstances; check current GMC guidance).

Plan your English language test early — ideally complete it before starting your MyIntealth verification so that both processes are running in parallel rather than sequentially. If your IELTS or OET result expires before you complete the PLAB process, you will need to resit the English language test, adding cost and delay to your timeline.

Step 4: Create or Log In to Your GMC Online Account

All PLAB registration and booking happens through the GMC Online portal. If you do not already have an account, create one at the GMC website. You will need:

  • A valid email address (use one you check regularly — all GMC communications go here)
  • A government-issued photo ID (passport strongly recommended, as it is accepted universally)
  • Personal details matching your MyIntealth profile exactly

Once your account is created, upload your identity documents and complete the initial application. The GMC will cross-reference your MyIntealth verification results once they arrive. Your GMC Online account will show the status of your application and, once you are cleared to book, will give you access to the exam booking system.

A common mistake is creating your GMC Online account with a different name spelling than appears on your MyIntealth profile or your identity documents. Even minor discrepancies (such as using a middle name in one place but not another, or a different transliteration of a name from Arabic or Hindi) can cause your application to be flagged and delayed. Use your full legal name exactly as it appears on your passport, consistently across all documents.

Step 5: Book Your PLAB 1 Sitting

Once your GMC Online application is approved and your eligibility confirmed, you can book your PLAB 1 sitting. This is done entirely online via GMC Online. Here is how to choose and secure your slot:

How to Choose the Best Test Centre

PLAB 1 is available at centres in the UK and at international locations including India (multiple cities), Nigeria (Lagos, Abuja), Pakistan (Islamabad, Karachi), Australia, Canada, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka. When choosing a centre, consider:

  • Distance and travel: Choose the closest available centre to minimise pre-exam fatigue from travel.
  • Availability: Popular centres fill very quickly — be flexible about location if your nearest centre is full.
  • Visa requirements: If your preferred centre is in another country, check visa requirements well in advance.
  • Sitting date: The same sitting dates apply across all centres worldwide in a given window, so date flexibility requires changing centre, not waiting for a different date.

What to Do If Your Preferred Date Is Full

If the sitting you want is fully booked, check other centres in your country or nearby countries. Occasionally, seats are released closer to the exam date when other candidates cancel. Check GMC Online regularly — there is no official waiting list, but released seats appear without notice and can be booked immediately by anyone who spots them. If no seat is available in any centre for that sitting, you will need to wait for the next quarterly window.

Step 6: Pay the Exam Fee

The PLAB 1 exam fee for 2025/2026 is £255. Payment is made online at the time of booking through the GMC Online portal. The GMC accepts major credit and debit cards. Keep a record of your payment confirmation and booking reference number — you will need these if you have any queries about your booking.

There is no fee reduction for repeat attempts. If you need to cancel your booking, check the GMC's cancellation policy carefully — fees are typically non-refundable if you cancel within a certain period before the exam date, and they are non-transferable to a different sitting without rebooking at the current fee. Plan your booking carefully to avoid unnecessary cancellations.

Confirmation and the Exam Admission Process

After booking, you will receive a booking confirmation email from the GMC. Approximately two to four weeks before your exam date, the GMC will send your official exam admission letter. This letter contains your candidate number, the test centre address, the reporting time, and instructions for what to bring. Print this letter and keep both a physical copy and a digital backup.

The admission letter is a mandatory document for entry to the test centre. Arrive at the centre without it and you may be refused entry with no recourse. If you have not received your admission letter two weeks before your exam, contact the GMC immediately.

What to Bring on the Day

  • Your official exam admission letter (printed)
  • A valid passport (the same passport used for your GMC Online application)
  • No electronic devices — phones, smart watches, and earphones must be left outside the examination hall or in a secure locker if provided
  • No revision materials — the test centre does not permit books, notes, or printed materials
  • Water and light snacks (for before the exam — check whether the centre allows these inside)

Technical Requirements for Online Booking

The GMC Online booking system is browser-based. Use a modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari) and ensure your internet connection is stable. Do not attempt to book on a mobile device — the booking interface works best on a desktop or laptop. If you experience technical issues during booking, the GMC has a support line. However, the best strategy is to avoid technical problems by using a reliable connection and device from the outset.

Screenshot or save your booking confirmation page immediately after completing your booking, before navigating away. If the page does not load correctly, check your email for a confirmation message before attempting to rebook — double-booking the same sitting will cause complications.

Common Registration Mistakes to Avoid

  • Mismatched names: Ensure your name is identical across your passport, MyIntealth profile, and GMC Online account.
  • Expired English language test: Check that your IELTS or OET result will still be within its two-year validity window on the date of your GMC application submission, not just on the date you take the exam.
  • Booking before verification is complete: You cannot book PLAB 1 until the GMC has received your MyIntealth verification results. Attempting to book before this step is complete wastes time and can cause confusion in your application.
  • Ignoring the booking opening time: International centre seats sell out within minutes. Know exactly when booking opens for your preferred sitting and be at your computer ready to act immediately.
  • Not saving confirmation emails: Every email from the GMC is important. Create a dedicated folder in your email account for all GMC correspondence and never delete it.
  • Cancelling without checking the refund policy: If your plans change, understand the cancellation terms before cancelling a booked sitting to avoid losing the full £255 fee.

After Booking: Your Preparation Timeline

Once your PLAB 1 sitting is booked, shift your focus entirely to preparation. Most candidates who pass PLAB 1 on their first attempt dedicate three to four months of structured study. Those who study for shorter periods tend to find themselves underprepared for the breadth of topics covered.

A typical preparation timeline looks like this: in the first four weeks, complete a diagnostic assessment using a question bank, identify your weakest domains from the MLA Content Map, and begin systematic topic review. In weeks five through ten, work through all major clinical domains using question banks and clinical guidelines, aiming to complete at least 1,500 practice questions. In weeks eleven and twelve, focus exclusively on timed full-paper mock exams under exam conditions, review all incorrect answers, and finalise weak areas.

Frequently Asked Questions About Registering for PLAB 1

What ID documents are accepted on the day of PLAB 1?

The GMC requires you to bring the same passport you used for your GMC Online application to the test centre. A passport is the only universally accepted identity document at all PLAB 1 centres, both in the UK and internationally. National identity cards, driving licences, or other forms of ID may not be accepted — check the specific requirements stated in your admission letter, as centre policies can vary. If your passport has expired or been renewed since you applied to the GMC, update your GMC Online profile and contact the GMC before the exam to ensure there are no issues on the day.

How many times can I attempt PLAB 1?

You are permitted a maximum of four attempts at PLAB 1. A fifth attempt may be granted if you have completed additional clinical experience or training that the GMC considers appropriate. If you have reached your fourth attempt, you must contact the GMC before booking to discuss eligibility for a further attempt. There is no provision for a fifth attempt simply due to illness on the day or other mitigating circumstances — mitigating circumstances affect the way the result is treated, but they do not add extra attempts beyond the standard limit.

Can I change my test centre after booking?

Changes to test centre bookings are subject to the GMC's booking amendment policy, which may involve cancelling your existing booking and rebooking. This could result in losing your original seat if the sitting you want is now full, and may incur a financial penalty depending on how close to the exam date you make the change. Contact the GMC as soon as you know you need to change your centre rather than waiting until close to the exam date.

What happens if I am unwell on the day of the exam?

If you are too unwell to sit your exam, do not attend the test centre. Contact the GMC as soon as possible and, if applicable, submit a mitigating circumstances application with supporting evidence (such as a medical certificate from your doctor). Turning up and attempting the exam while genuinely unwell is unlikely to produce a good result and the attempt will count towards your attempt limit regardless. If your mitigating circumstances application is accepted, the GMC may allow the attempt not to count or may give you priority booking for the next sitting.

Conclusion

Registering for PLAB 1 is a multi-stage process that requires careful coordination between MyIntealth verification, English language testing, and GMC Online booking. The candidates who navigate it most smoothly are those who start early, keep meticulous records, and treat every deadline seriously. Now that you know exactly how to register for PLAB 1, you can build your personal timeline and start the process with confidence.
